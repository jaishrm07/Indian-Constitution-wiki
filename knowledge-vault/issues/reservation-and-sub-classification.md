## What happened

Contemporary debates over reservation design and sub-classification continue to test how the Constitution balances equality, representation, and state capacity to target disadvantage.

## Constitutional question

How far can the state refine reservation categories while remaining faithful to constitutional equality and the structure of affirmative action?

## Why it matters

These debates affect public employment, educational opportunity, and how the Constitution understands justice in a socially stratified society.

## Canonical site note

The constitutional frame
Reservation design is not a departure from equality but part of one constitutional approach to equality. The harder disputes concern category design, internal differentiation, and the justification for specific state choices.
What readers should watch
The key issues are classification logic, evidentiary basis, representational claims, and whether the framework remains consistent with constitutional structure.
