# Summary

The 2025 Article 200 ruling made prolonged gubernatorial inaction a constitutional problem, not just a political delay. The live dispute now concerns how far Article 201 can be used without becoming a pocket veto by another name.

## Core constitutional question

Can a Governor sit on a bill, reserve it without adequate reason, or otherwise postpone legislative effect in a way that defeats representative government?

## Timeline

- the 2025 judgment narrows the space for bill-silence tactics
- the debate continues over timelines, Presidential handling, and judicial review
- state-level bill disputes remain constitutionally sensitive

## Institutional actors

- Governor
- State government
- State legislature
- President
- Supreme Court

## Source gaps

- the exact implementation consequences of the 2025 ruling should be tracked against later references and follow-up litigation
- if Parliament clarifies timelines, that text should be added to `raw/`

## Open questions

- Does Article 201 permit any form of prolonged silence after a bill is re-passed?
- How should constitutional timelines be enforced without erasing the Governor's limited role?
