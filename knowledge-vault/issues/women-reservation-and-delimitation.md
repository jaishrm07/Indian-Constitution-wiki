# Summary

The 106th Amendment gave women reservation a constitutional home, but its real-world effect is tied to delimitation and future census-linked implementation. The issue is therefore about timing, design, and constitutional sequencing, not just representation in the abstract.

## Core constitutional question

How should the Constitution translate the new reservation guarantee into seats without creating avoidable conflict between representation, delimitation, and the existing electoral timetable?

## Timeline

- the 106th Amendment is enacted
- implementation is tied to future delimitation after census figures
- debate continues over how the operational law should work in practice

## Institutional actors

- Parliament
- Election Commission of India
- Union government
- State governments

## Source gaps

- the implementation law and any notification timeline should be tracked in `raw/`
- rotation rules and Delhi-specific sequencing need a dedicated note once they are clarified

## Open questions

- How will the one-third design be operationalised across different tiers of representation?
- Will future delimitation and reservation rotation be handled together or in stages?
