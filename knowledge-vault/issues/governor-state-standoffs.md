## What happened

Repeated clashes between governors and elected state governments keep raising questions about federalism, discretionary power, and the proper constitutional route for testing political majorities.

## Constitutional question

When can constitutional functionaries intervene in state politics without displacing democratic accountability or distorting federal balance?

## Why it matters

These disputes affect how state governments function, when assemblies meet, how bills move, and whether constitutional offices are used neutrally or strategically.

## Canonical site note

The constitutional frame
Not every political impasse justifies extraordinary intervention. Constitutional design generally prefers floor tests, reasoned decisionmaking, and respect for elected institutions over unilateral institutional assertions.
What readers should watch
The key constitutional signals are whether the dispute concerns discretionary action, legislative majority, or a claim that ordinary government can no longer function according to the Constitution.
