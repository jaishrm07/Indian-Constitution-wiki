# Summary

The simultaneous elections debate is often framed as an efficiency question, but constitutionally it is a structural redesign question. It raises issues of federal autonomy, dissolution mechanics, accountability cycles, and the limits of amendment power.

## Core constitutional question

Can election cycles be synchronised without distorting the Constitution's assumptions about representative government, state autonomy, and the relationship between electoral timing and political accountability?

## Timeline

- proposals gain traction as a governance reform idea
- constitutional scrutiny shifts attention to amendment depth and federal consequences
- current debate remains unresolved
