## What happened

Periodic calls for deep structural reform continue to revive the question of how far Parliament can reshape the constitutional order through Article 368.

## Constitutional question

Can Parliament pursue major institutional redesign through the amendment process without crossing the boundary set by the basic structure doctrine?

## Why it matters

This issue affects debates on institutional balance, electoral structure, federal design, and the durability of constitutional safeguards.

## Canonical site note

The constitutional frame
Article 368 creates the formal route for amendment, but formal validity does not end the inquiry. The deeper constitutional question is whether the proposed change preserves the document's identity as interpreted through doctrine.
What readers should watch
The difficult cases are not routine textual updates. They are proposals that alter institutional relationships, democratic guarantees, judicial review, or the federal compact.
