## Summary

The One Hundred and Fourth Amendment extended the SC/ST reservation period in legislatures and allowed Anglo-Indian nomination to lapse on the constitutional timetable.

## What changed

It amended Article 334 to extend the duration of seat reservation for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes by ten years while keeping the Anglo-Indian special representation provision on its expiry track.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The One Hundred and Fourth Amendment is not a broad doctrinal amendment, but it matters for how the Constitution manages temporary representation arrangements. It shows how transitional representation can be renewed, then allowed to expire, without rewriting the larger equality framework.
Reading note
Read this page alongside Article 334 and the reservations topic. It is a reminder that constitutional representation sometimes works through sunset clauses, not permanent rules.
