## Summary

The One Hundred and Third Amendment created the economically weaker sections reservation framework in education and public employment.

## What changed

It inserted clauses (6) into Articles 15 and 16, allowing up to ten percent reservation for economically weaker sections outside the existing SC, ST, and OBC framework.

- [[../articles/article-15|Article 15]]
- [[../articles/article-16|Article 16]]
- [[../parts/part-iii|Part III - Fundamental Rights]]

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The One Hundred and Third Amendment is a major equality amendment because it does not simply expand an older reservation category. It creates a separate constitutional route based on economic disadvantage and changes how readers think about substantive equality.
Reading note
Read this amendment with Articles 15 and 16 together. It is the clearest example of the Constitution building a new reservation logic directly into the equality chapter rather than borrowing from older categories.
