## Summary

The Ninety-First Amendment tightened the constitutional rules around the size of ministries and office-holding across legislatures.

## What changed

It limited the size of the Council of Ministers and strengthened anti-defection-related disqualification rules for ministerial office.

- [[../articles/article-75|Article 75]]

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The Ninety-First Amendment is one of the most practical constitutional reforms of the coalition era. It tries to make executive government leaner and defection less rewarding.
