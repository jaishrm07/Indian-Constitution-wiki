## Summary

The Seventy-Seventh Amendment restored an explicit constitutional basis for reservation in promotion for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes.

## What changed

It inserted Article 16(4A), allowing reservation in promotion with consequential seniority for SC/ST candidates when the state considered such measures necessary.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The SeventySeventh Amendment shows that equality in public employment is not only about entry into office. It also shapes advancement, hierarchy, and how the state measures substantive representation over time.
Reading note
Read this amendment with Article 16 and the broader reservations topic. It is one of the clearest examples of the Constitution explicitly responding to a doctrinal gap in service jurisprudence.
