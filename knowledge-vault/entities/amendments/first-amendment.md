## Summary

The First Amendment significantly altered the early balance between rights protection and state power, especially around speech and social reform.

## What changed

It introduced changes affecting free speech restrictions and property-related issues, and responded to some of the earliest constitutional litigation after commencement.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The First Amendment shows how quickly constitutional politics moved from adoption to contestation. It also reminds readers that rights interpretation and amendment politics developed together from the beginning.
