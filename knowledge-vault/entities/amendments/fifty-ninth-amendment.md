## Summary

The Fifty-Ninth Amendment adapted emergency powers for the Punjab crisis by tightening the state's special emergency rules and narrowing suspension of certain rights.

## What changed

It amended Articles 352, 358, and 359 and inserted Article 359A to create a Punjab-specific emergency framework that preserved Article 21 from the suspension logic.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The FiftyNinth Amendment is important because it shows that emergency law is not onesizefitsall. The Punjab context forced Parliament to rework the relationship between security, rights, and constitutional temporariness.
Reading note
This amendment belongs with the Emergency Provisions chapter, but it is narrower than the 1975 Emergency logic. It is best read as a targeted constitutional response to a specific security crisis.
