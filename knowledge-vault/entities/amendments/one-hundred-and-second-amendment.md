## Summary

The One Hundred and Second Amendment constitutionally entrenched the National Commission for Backward Classes and created the Central List of socially and educationally backward classes.

## What changed

It inserted Article 338B, Article 342A, and the Article 366 definition of socially and educationally backward classes, shifting backward-class identification into the Constitution and triggering a central-state list debate.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The One Hundred and Second Amendment is important because it moved backwardclass identification from a mainly statutory and administrative space into the constitutional text itself. That move made the dispute over lists much more consequential.
Reading note
This amendment is best read together with the One Hundred and Fifth Amendment. The two pages form a constitutional pair: first the Central List was entrenched, then state listmaking had to be restored.
