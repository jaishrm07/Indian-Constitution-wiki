## Summary

Often described as a transformative Emergency-era amendment, the Forty-Second Amendment sought major changes to constitutional structure and state power.

## What changed

It touched multiple institutional questions and became a key reference point for later debates about concentrated power, amendment limits, and constitutional identity.

- [[../articles/article-368|Article 368]]

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The FortySecond Amendment is central to understanding why later courts and political actors treated amendment power with suspicion. It is one of the clearest demonstrations of how constitutional form can be used to pursue structural transformation.
