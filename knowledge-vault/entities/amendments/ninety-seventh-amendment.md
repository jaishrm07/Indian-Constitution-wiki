## Summary

The Ninety-Seventh Amendment constitutionalised cooperative societies and gave them a federal-democratic frame.

## What changed

It inserted Article 43B, added the right to form cooperative societies in Article 19, and created Part IXB for cooperative governance.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The Ninety-Seventh Amendment shows how the Constitution can constitutionalise institutions that were previously treated as ordinary statutory creatures. It is a useful bridge between rights, devolution, and institutional design.
