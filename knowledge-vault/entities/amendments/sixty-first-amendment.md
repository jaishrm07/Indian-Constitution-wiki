## Summary

The Sixty-First Amendment lowered the voting age and expanded democratic participation.

## What changed

It changed the franchise threshold from 21 years to 18 years for elections to the House of the People and state legislatures.

## Affected provisions

- [[../articles/article-326|Article 326]]

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The Sixty-First Amendment is a straightforward but important democratic reform. It widened participation and changed the generational shape of electoral politics.
