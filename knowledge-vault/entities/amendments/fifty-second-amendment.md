## Summary

The Fifty-Second Amendment introduced the anti-defection framework and changed the logic of legislative loyalty in India.

## What changed

It inserted the Tenth Schedule and created a constitutional mechanism for disqualification on the ground of defection.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The Fifty-Second Amendment is the starting point for modern anti-defection analysis. It affects how parties, legislatures, and government stability are understood in constitutional practice.
