## Summary

The Twenty-Fourth Amendment reaffirmed Parliament's power to amend the Constitution and is inseparable from later basic-structure debate.

## What changed

It clarified that Parliament could amend any provision of the Constitution and also required presidential assent to amendment bills.

## Affected provisions

- [[../articles/article-368|Article 368]]

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The Twenty-Fourth Amendment is the key textual response to early judicial skepticism about amendment power. It is one of the foundation stones for later basic-structure litigation and the constitutional politics of Article 368.
