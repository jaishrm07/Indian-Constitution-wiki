## Summary

The Eighty-Sixth Amendment strengthened the constitutional commitment to education and changed the rights framework around childhood learning.

## What changed

It inserted Article 21A, changed Article 45, and linked education to a specific fundamental-rights obligation for the state.

## Affected provisions

- [[../articles/article-21a|Article 21A]]
- [[../articles/article-45|Article 45]]

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The Eighty-Sixth Amendment is the pivot from aspiration to enforceability in education rights. It makes childhood education a constitutional guarantee rather than only a policy commitment.
