## Summary

The Ninety-Ninth Amendment created the National Judicial Appointments Commission and tried to replace the collegium-style appointments process with a commission-based model.

## What changed

It inserted Articles 124A, 124B, and 124C and amended related judicial appointment provisions, making judicial appointments and transfers a central constitutional design question.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
The NinetyNinth Amendment is the modern judiciary amendment that every constitutional reader has to understand. It became central to the larger question of whether Parliament can redesign judicial appointments without undermining judicial independence.
Reading note
Read it with the judicial appointments articles and the basicstructure topic. The amendment is not just about staffing the courts; it is about who gets to shape the constitutional conditions of adjudication.
