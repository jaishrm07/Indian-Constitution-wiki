## Summary

The Court gave a major exposition of minority educational rights and the extent to which the state may regulate educational institutions without destroying constitutional autonomy.

## Facts and issue

How Articles 29 and 30 protect minority educational institutions and what kinds of regulation remain constitutionally permissible.

## Holding

Minority institutions retain a constitutionally protected right to establish and administer educational institutions, but that autonomy exists alongside regulatory authority aimed at educational standards and fairness.

## Constitutional significance

T.M.A. Pai Foundation is a landmark for minority rights, educational autonomy, and the relationship between pluralism and regulation.
