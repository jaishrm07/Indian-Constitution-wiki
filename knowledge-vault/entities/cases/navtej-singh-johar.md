## Summary

The Court decriminalised consensual same-sex relations and grounded the decision in dignity, equality, autonomy, and constitutional morality.

## Facts and issue

Whether Section 377 could continue to criminalise consensual adult same-sex intimacy.

## Holding

The Court read Section 377 down so that consensual adult same-sex conduct is no longer punishable, recognising the constitutional rights of LGBTQ+ persons.

## Constitutional significance

Navtej Singh Johar is a landmark dignity and equality case that reoriented Indian constitutional law toward personal autonomy and constitutional morality.

## Canonical site note

Why the case remains central
The judgment matters because it extends constitutional protection to a historically stigmatised class through the language of dignity, autonomy, and equal citizenship.
Doctrinal line
It ties together equality, liberty, expression, and privacy in a way that makes Indian rights doctrine more internally coherent than a compartmentalised reading would allow.
Longterm impact
Navtej Singh Johar now informs constitutional arguments about bodily autonomy, antidiscrimination, and the limits of moral policing by the state.
