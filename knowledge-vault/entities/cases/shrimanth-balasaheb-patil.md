## Summary

The Supreme Court addressed Speaker's power under the Tenth Schedule when legislators resigned to avoid disqualification, and set timelines for Speakers to decide disqualification petitions.

## Facts and issue

Whether a Speaker must decide disqualification petitions within a reasonable time, and whether resignation filed before a disqualification petition is accepted extinguishes the petition.

## Holding

The Speaker is constitutionally bound to decide disqualification petitions within a reasonable time and courts can prescribe timelines. A valid resignation accepted before the disqualification petition is decided removes the member from the House and renders the petition infructuous.

## Constitutional significance

This case set important procedural limits on Speaker delay in anti-defection cases and clarified the relationship between voluntary resignation and disqualification under the Tenth Schedule.

## Canonical site note

Why the case remains central
The judgment arose from the Karnataka political crisis in which rebel legislators resigned from the Assembly to precipitate a government collapse, forcing the Court to resolve the procedural collision between resignation and disqualification. By holding that courts may impose timelines on Speakers, the case pierced the shield of exclusive jurisdictional finality that Speakers had long asserted under the Tenth Schedule.
Longterm impact
Shrimanth Balasaheb Patil became a reference point in every subsequent dispute where a Speaker sat on disqualification petitions while the political situation beneath them shifted. The ruling's clarification that a voluntarily accepted resignation renders a disqualification petition infructuous added a new strategic dimension to how legislators and party managers approach floor management and defection crises.
How to find the judgment

The judgment clarifies the resignationdisqualification sequence and the timeline for Speaker decisions; read alongside Keisham Meghachandra Singh (2020) 2 SCC 598 for the full picture on Speaker delay.
