## Summary

The Court held that convicted legislators face immediate disqualification and cannot continue in office merely because an appeal is pending.

## Facts and issue

Whether Section 8(4) of the Representation of the People Act could postpone disqualification after conviction.

## Holding

The Court struck down the protective clause and held that disqualification operates immediately upon conviction.

## Constitutional significance

Lily Thomas is the leading disqualification case for criminalisation of politics and electoral accountability.
