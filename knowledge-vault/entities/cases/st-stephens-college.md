## Summary

The Court protected minority educational autonomy while allowing regulation that did not destroy the institution's character.

## Facts and issue

How far minority colleges may shape admissions without violating equality norms or university regulation.

## Holding

The Court accepted that Article 30 protects minority institutions' admissions autonomy, but that autonomy must operate within fair and academically legitimate standards.

## Constitutional significance

St. Stephen's is a foundational Article 30 admissions case and a major precursor to later minority-education doctrine.
