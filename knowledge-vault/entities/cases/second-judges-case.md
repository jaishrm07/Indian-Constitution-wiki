## Summary

A nine-judge bench reinterpreted the appointments clauses to give primacy to the judiciary's institutional opinion in the selection of judges.

## Facts and issue

How should Articles 124 and 217 be read when the executive and judiciary disagree over the appointment of judges.

## Holding

The Court substantially narrowed executive discretion and held that the Chief Justice of India's institutional opinion would carry primacy in appointments.

## Constitutional significance

The judgment is foundational to the collegium system and to modern constitutional debates on judicial independence and accountability.

## Canonical site note

Why the case remains central
This is the modern starting point for India's judgeappointments system. It changed the constitutional meaning of consultation in a way that still structures every appointments debate.
Longterm impact
Arguments about delay, transparency, reform, and institutional balance all operate in the shadow of the Second Judges Case.
