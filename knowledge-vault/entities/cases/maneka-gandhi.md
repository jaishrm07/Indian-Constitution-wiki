## Summary

The Court transformed Article 21 by holding that any procedure depriving liberty must be fair, just, and reasonable.

## Facts and issue

Whether procedure established by law in Article 21 could be read narrowly as any validly enacted procedure, regardless of fairness.

## Holding

Articles 14, 19, and 21 form an interconnected triad, and liberty-depriving procedures must meet standards of fairness and non-arbitrariness.

## Constitutional significance

Maneka Gandhi shifted Indian constitutional law toward substantive due process and expanded the practical reach of Article 21.

## Canonical site note

Why the case remains central
The judgment is a turning point in Indian rights jurisprudence because it rejected narrow formalism and moved toward a more substantive conception of liberty and fairness.
Longterm impact
Later privacy, dignity, travel, and procedural fairness cases build on the doctrinal opening created by Maneka Gandhi.
