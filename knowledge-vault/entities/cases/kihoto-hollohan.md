## Summary

The Court upheld the anti-defection law while allowing limited judicial review of the Speaker's decisions.

## Facts and issue

Whether the Tenth Schedule's anti-defection framework and the Speaker's adjudicatory role were constitutionally valid.

## Holding

The Tenth Schedule was largely upheld, but judicial review was not entirely excluded.

## Constitutional significance

Kihoto Hollohan remains central to understanding anti-defection, legislative stability, and the constitutional role of presiding officers.
