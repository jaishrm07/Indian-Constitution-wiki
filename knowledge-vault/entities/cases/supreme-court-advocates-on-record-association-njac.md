## Summary

The Court struck down the Ninety-ninth Amendment and the NJAC Act, holding that the new appointments structure damaged judicial independence.

## Facts and issue

Whether Parliament could replace the collegium with the National Judicial Appointments Commission without violating the Constitution's basic structure.

## Holding

The amendment and the accompanying statute were invalidated because the Court held that judicial primacy and independence in the appointments process were constitutionally protected.

## Constitutional significance

The judgment remains the defining constitutional decision on judicial appointments, court independence, and the limits of institutional redesign by amendment.
