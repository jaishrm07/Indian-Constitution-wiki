## Summary

The Court treated bribery-linked speech and vote claims as covered by parliamentary privilege immunity under Article 105(2).

## Facts and issue

Whether MPs can claim constitutional immunity from prosecution for accepting bribes connected to speech or voting in Parliament.

## Holding

The majority accepted privilege-based immunity from criminal prosecution for acts tied to speech or vote in the House.

## Constitutional significance

This remains a foundational parliamentary-privilege case, even though later jurisprudence has narrowed its immunity logic.
