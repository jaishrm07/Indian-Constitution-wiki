## Summary

The Court upheld the early preventive detention framework and took a narrow view of how Article 21 interacted with other fundamental rights.

## Facts and issue

Whether preventive detention could be tested through a broader reading of liberty, fairness, and the interrelationship between fundamental rights.

## Holding

The majority upheld the detention law and read Article 21 in a compartmentalised way, leaving the broader liberty critique to later cases and the dissent.

## Constitutional significance

A.K. Gopalan is the starting point for India’s modern liberty jurisprudence because later cases define themselves against its narrow formalism.

## Canonical site note

Why the case remains central
This is the foundational early liberty case. It shows how narrowly the Court initially read constitutional freedom before later doctrine widened the scope of Articles 14, 19, and 21.
Doctrinal line
The judgment is important because it treated preventive detention as a separate constitutional domain rather than part of a broader fairness inquiry. That approach later came under sustained criticism.
Longterm impact
Later decisions on procedural fairness, detention, and personal liberty are easiest to understand as a response to A.K. Gopalan’s formalism.
