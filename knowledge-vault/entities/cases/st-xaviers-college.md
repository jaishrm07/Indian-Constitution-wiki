## Summary

The Court gave a foundational reading of minority educational autonomy under Article 30 while acknowledging that regulation is possible if it does not destroy constitutional protection.

## Facts and issue

How far can the state regulate minority educational institutions without undermining the right of minorities to establish and administer institutions of their choice.

## Holding

The Court held that regulatory measures are permissible, but the state cannot use regulation to take away the core autonomy protected by Article 30.

## Constitutional significance

St. Xavier's remains a foundational judgment on minority rights, institutional autonomy, and the constitutional shape of educational regulation.
