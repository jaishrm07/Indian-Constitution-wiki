## Summary

The Court held that laws placed in the Ninth Schedule after the basic-structure doctrine took shape remain subject to constitutional review.

## Facts and issue

Whether Ninth Schedule placement can immunise a law from basic-structure scrutiny.

## Holding

Laws inserted into the Ninth Schedule after Kesavananda remain open to challenge if they damage the Constitution's basic structure.

## Constitutional significance

I.R. Coelho is the modern 9th Schedule case and one of the clearest applications of basic-structure review to immunised laws.

## Canonical site note

Why the case remains central
I.R. Coelho closes a loophole that could otherwise have turned the Ninth Schedule into a safe harbour for unconstitutional laws.
Doctrinal line
The case makes the basicstructure doctrine operational in a concrete setting: textually protected laws still cannot damage the Constitution’s core identity.
Longterm impact
Any serious study of constitutional immunities, amendment design, or rightsversuslegislativeprotection now has to account for this decision.
