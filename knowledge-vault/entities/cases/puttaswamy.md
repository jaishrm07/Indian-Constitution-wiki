## Summary

A nine-judge bench held that privacy is a constitutionally protected right rooted in liberty, dignity, and autonomy.

## Facts and issue

Whether the Constitution protects privacy as a fundamental right and how that right fits within existing rights doctrine.

## Holding

Privacy is protected under the Constitution and is tied to dignity, liberty, and individual autonomy.

## Constitutional significance

Puttaswamy reshaped constitutional discourse on technology, state databases, surveillance, and personal choice.
