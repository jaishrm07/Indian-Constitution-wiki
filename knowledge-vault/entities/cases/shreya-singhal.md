## Summary

The Court struck down Section 66A of the IT Act and reinforced constitutional protection for online speech.

## Facts and issue

Whether vague and overbroad restrictions on online communication were compatible with Article 19(1)(a) and the constitutional scheme of permissible restrictions.

## Holding

Section 66A was unconstitutional because it imposed vague and chilling restrictions on free speech.

## Constitutional significance

Shreya Singhal remains the leading constitutional decision on internet speech and overbroad speech regulation in India.
