## Summary

The Court invalidated a communal admissions policy and forced the Constitution to clarify how equality and affirmative action fit together.

## Facts and issue

Whether caste- and community-based quotas in state educational admissions could survive the equality guarantees in Part III.

## Holding

The Court struck down the admissions classification as unconstitutional, which helped trigger the First Amendment and later affirmative-action text.

## Constitutional significance

Champakam Dorairajan is the first great equality and reservation case because it exposed the gap between formal equality and social justice design.

## Canonical site note

Why the case remains central
The decision is the constitutional starting point for the reservation story in education. It made clear that state classifications in admissions could not be treated as ordinary policy choices.
Doctrinal line
The immediate constitutional consequence was the First Amendment and the later framing of Article 15(4), which allowed the Constitution to speak more clearly about substantive equality.
Longterm impact
Every modern argument about educational reservations has to account for this case’s insistence that equality provisions mean something concrete at the institutional level.
