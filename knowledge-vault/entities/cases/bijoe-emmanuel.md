## Summary

The Court protected Jehovah's Witness students who respectfully refused to sing the national anthem on sincere religious grounds.

## Facts and issue

Can students be punished for standing respectfully but declining to sing the national anthem because of religious conscience.

## Holding

The Court held that compelled singing would violate conscience and religious freedom, so the students could not be punished.

## Constitutional significance

Bijoe Emmanuel is a classic conscience and secularism case that protects tolerance against state coercion.
