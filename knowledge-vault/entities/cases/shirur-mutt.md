## Summary

The Court gave foundational shape to Articles 25 and 26 by distinguishing protected religious matters from secular administration open to regulation.

## Facts and issue

How far the state could regulate religious institutions and what Article 26 protects as matters of religion and denominational autonomy.

## Holding

The judgment treated freedom of religion and denominational management as constitutionally protected, while accepting that secular administration of property and institutions could be regulated by law.

## Constitutional significance

Shirur Mutt remains one of the starting points for Indian constitutional law on religious denomination, essential practice reasoning, and the boundary between religion and regulation.
