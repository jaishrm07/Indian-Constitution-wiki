## Summary

The Court held that States may sub-classify Scheduled Caste reservation benefits to address internal inequities within the larger constitutional category.

## Facts and issue

Whether the Constitution permits sub-classification within the Scheduled Castes for distributive fairness, or whether such internal differentiation violates the equality framework governing reservation.

## Holding

The Court permitted sub-classification and treated distributive re-ordering within the constitutional category as compatible with the equality code, subject to constitutional discipline.

## Constitutional significance

Davinder Singh is now central to contemporary debates on reservation design, internal disadvantage, and the relationship between formal classification and substantive equality.
