## Summary

The Court linked the right to education to Article 21 and treated elementary education as constitutionally central to dignified life.

## Facts and issue

Does the Constitution protect education as part of the right to life and how should that right relate to directive-principle commitments.

## Holding

The Court recognized education, especially at the elementary level, as constitutionally grounded through Article 21 read with directive principles.

## Constitutional significance

The judgment helped lay the doctrinal path that later culminated in Article 21A and the constitutionalization of elementary education.

## Canonical site note

Why the case remains central
The judgment is an important bridge between rights and directive principles. It shows how social rights arguments entered constitutional doctrine before the text was later amended.
Longterm impact
Modern debates over school access, state obligations, and Article 21A make more sense when read against Unni Krishnan's attempt to constitutionalize education.
