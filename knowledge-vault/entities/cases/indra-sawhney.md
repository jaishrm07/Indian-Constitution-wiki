## Summary

The Court gave a foundational interpretation of reservation policy under the equality code, especially Article 16.

## Facts and issue

How should constitutional equality accommodate reservation and what limits apply to affirmative action in public employment.

## Holding

Reservation was upheld within a structured equality framework, and the Court articulated major limits and principles for reservation design.

## Constitutional significance

Indra Sawhney remains central to constitutional debates on equality, backwardness, representation, and reservation limits.

Why the case remains central
This is one of the most influential judgments on substantive equality in India. It continues to shape how constitutional arguments about representation and reservation are framed.

Later reservation policy, classification disputes, and amendment debates all operate in the shadow of Indra Sawhney.
