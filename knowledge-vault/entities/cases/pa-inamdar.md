## Summary

The Court limited the state's ability to impose reservation policy on private unaided professional institutions while preserving regulatory power over fairness and standards.

## Facts and issue

Can the State require private unaided professional colleges to follow its reservation policy for admissions.

## Holding

The Court rejected compulsory state-imposed quotas in private unaided institutions and emphasized institutional autonomy subject to anti-exploitation regulation.

## Constitutional significance

P.A. Inamdar is central to private and minority professional-education autonomy after T.M.A. Pai.
