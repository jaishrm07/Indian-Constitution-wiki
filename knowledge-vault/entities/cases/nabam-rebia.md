## Summary

The Court limited the Governor's ability to use Article 174 in a way that displaces the constitutional scheme of responsible government in a State.

## Facts and issue

Whether the Governor could advance an Assembly session and shape legislative business in the circumstances of a political crisis in Arunachal Pradesh.

## Holding

The Governor's constitutional position does not allow open-ended political discretion over legislative sessions and agenda-setting. Formal powers under the Constitution must be read in light of cabinet responsibility and democratic structure.

## Constitutional significance

Nabam Rebia is now one of the key cases for understanding Governor discretion, assembly sessions, and the constitutional limits of intervention in state politics.
