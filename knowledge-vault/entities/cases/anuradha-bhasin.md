## Summary

The Court treated internet shutdowns and publication restrictions as constitutionally serious limits on speech, reviewability, and administrative legality.

## Facts and issue

How constitutional free-speech principles apply when the state restricts internet access and communication through executive orders during a security situation.

## Holding

Restrictions on internet access and related speech interests must remain subject to legality, publication, necessity, and judicial review rather than operating as opaque executive control.

## Constitutional significance

Anuradha Bhasin is a key case for modern free-speech doctrine because it translates constitutional speech and legality concerns into the digital communications environment.

Why the case remains central
Any constitutional debate about internet shutdowns, digital public discourse, or the procedural legality of communication restrictions now runs through this judgment.
Longterm impact
The case reinforced the idea that digital restrictions are still constitutional restrictions and cannot evade ordinary legality and review merely because they operate through communications infrastructure.
