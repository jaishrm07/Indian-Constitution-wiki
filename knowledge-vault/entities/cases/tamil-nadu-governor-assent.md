## Summary

The Court treated prolonged inaction on state bills as a constitutional problem and clarified the limits of gubernatorial discretion under Articles 200 and 201.

## Facts and issue

Can a Governor withhold action on bills, reserve repassed bills for the President without constitutional discipline, or turn delay into an effective veto.

## Holding

The Court sharply constrained gubernatorial inaction and clarified that assent-related powers must operate within a constitutionally reviewable framework.

## Constitutional significance

The ruling is now the leading authority on Governor assent, state-legislature autonomy, and judicial control of constitutional inaction.
