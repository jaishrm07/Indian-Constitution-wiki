## Summary

The Court reaffirmed that voters are entitled to know basic information about candidates, including criminal antecedents and other core disclosures.

## Facts and issue

Whether the voter’s right to know candidate antecedents and basic qualifications could be diluted by statute.

## Holding

The Court struck down the attempt to dilute disclosure and confirmed that candidate transparency is part of meaningful democratic choice.

## Constitutional significance

This is the leading candidate-disclosure case for electoral transparency and the constitutional right to information in elections.

Why the case remains central
The case constitutionalized the idea that a voter cannot make a meaningful choice without basic information about candidates.
Longterm impact
It still anchors later disclosure fights, including candidate affidavits, electoral transparency, and the broader righttoknow logic in election law.
