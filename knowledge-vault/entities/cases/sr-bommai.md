# Summary

S.R. Bommai is the key modern limitation on the misuse of Article 356. It subjects President's Rule proclamations to meaningful judicial review and reinforces federalism as a constitutional value.

## Facts and issue

The case asked whether proclamations under Article 356 were effectively beyond review. The Court rejected that understanding.

## Holding

Article 356 proclamations are reviewable, and legislative majority should ordinarily be tested on the assembly floor.

## Constitutional significance

Bommai converted a historically abuse-prone emergency power into one constrained by constitutional reasoning, judicial review, and democratic process.
