## Summary

The Court required local-body elections to be held on time and treated delay as constitutionally impermissible.

## Facts and issue

Can municipal elections be delayed beyond the constitutional timetable because of administrative or political delay.

## Holding

The Court held that timely elections are a constitutional obligation and that the State Election Commission must ensure they happen within the prescribed period.

## Constitutional significance

Kishansing Tomar is the leading local-self-government elections case and a key authority on Part IX-A timing.
