## Summary

The Court upheld the constitutional space for reservations in local bodies but required careful justification for backward-class reservation and rejected a mechanical carryover of legislative reservation logic.

## Facts and issue

How the Constitution's local-government provisions should balance democratic participation, representation, and equality when seats in panchayats and municipalities are reserved.

## Holding

Reservations for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, and women in local bodies were sustained, but backward-class reservations required stronger constitutional justification and empirical care.

## Constitutional significance

The case is central to understanding how decentralisation, representation, and equality operate differently in local self-government than in legislatures or public employment.
