## Summary

The Court treated the conduct of elections as a constitutionally protected process and clarified the institutional importance of Article 324.

## Facts and issue

How far courts should interfere with the electoral process and how the Election Commission's constitutional powers should be understood.

## Holding

The judgment emphasized the constitutional significance of election administration and the need to protect the continuity of the election process while recognizing the Commission's institutional role.

## Constitutional significance

Mohinder Singh Gill remains a foundational case for election law, Article 324, and the constitutional logic behind limiting midstream judicial interference in elections.
