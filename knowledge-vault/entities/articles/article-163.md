## Summary

Article 163 provides for a Council of Ministers to aid and advise the Governor, while preserving limited areas of constitutional discretion.

## Official text

There shall be a Council of Ministers with the Chief Minister at the head to aid and advise the Governor in the exercise of his functions, except in so far as he is by or under this Constitution required to exercise his functions or any of them in his discretion.

## Plain-English meaning

The Governor is not meant to function as a parallel political executive. Article 163 assumes that the Governor ordinarily acts with ministerial advice, except where the Constitution clearly allows discretionary action.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Many governorstate conflicts are really disputes about how wide Article 163 discretion can be. The provision helps distinguish ordinary constitutional advice from exceptional discretion.
How courts use it
Courts return to Article 163 when deciding whether a Governor acted within a genuine constitutional discretion or displaced democratic government by treating discretion too broadly.
