## Summary

Article 28 governs religious instruction and worship in educational institutions, especially where State funding or recognition is involved.

## Official text

No religious instruction shall be provided in any educational institution wholly maintained out of State funds, subject to the constitutional exception for institutions administered by the State but established under an endowment or trust requiring religious instruction. No person attending a recognised or aided educational institution shall be required to take part in religious instruction or worship without consent.

## Plain-English meaning

The Constitution sharply limits religious instruction in institutions wholly maintained by the state and protects students from being compelled into religious instruction or worship in aided or recognized institutions without consent.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 28 is where secularism, education, and consent meet. It matters in disputes over public schooling, aided institutions, and the boundary between institutional character and student compulsion.
How courts and institutions use it
The provision is used to distinguish between institutional identity, state funding, and the constitutional requirement that participation in religious instruction or worship not be coerced.
