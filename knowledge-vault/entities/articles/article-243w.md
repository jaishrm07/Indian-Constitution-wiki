## Summary

Article 243W allows state legislatures to devolve powers and responsibilities to municipalities as institutions of self-government.

## Official text

Subject to the provisions of this Constitution, the Legislature of a State may, by law, endow the Municipalities with such powers and authority as may be necessary to enable them to function as institutions of self-government and such law may contain provisions for the devolution of powers and responsibilities upon Municipalities, subject to such conditions as may be specified therein, with respect to the preparation of plans for economic development and social justice and the performance of functions including those in relation to the matters listed in the Twelfth Schedule.

## Plain-English meaning

This Article is the constitutional hinge for urban local government. It explains how municipalities can be given real governing functions instead of being treated as only administrative extensions of the state.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Urban governance depends on constitutional design as much as on municipal statutes. Article 243W is one of the clearest textual anchors for understanding city government as a constitutional subject.
How to read it
Read it with Part IXA, the Twelfth Schedule, and the SeventyFourth Amendment. That combination shows how the Constitution extends decentralised democracy into the urban sphere.
