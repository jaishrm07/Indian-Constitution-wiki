## Summary

Article 324 vests the superintendence, direction, and control of elections in the Election Commission.

## Official text

The superintendence, direction and control of the preparation of the electoral rolls for, and the conduct of, all elections to Parliament and to the Legislature of every State and of elections to the offices of President and Vice-President held under this Constitution shall be vested in a Commission referred to in this Constitution as the Election Commission.

## Plain-English meaning

The constitutional system does not leave elections entirely to ordinary politics. Article 324 gives the Election Commission a constitutional role in managing elections and electoral rolls.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 324 anchors the constitutional status of election administration. It matters whenever disputes arise over institutional independence, electoral procedure, or the scope of Election Commission power.
How courts use it
Courts use Article 324 when examining the Commission's powers, its relationship with legislation, and the constitutional demands of free and fair elections.
