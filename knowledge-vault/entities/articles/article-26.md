## Summary

Article 26 protects the right of religious denominations to manage religious affairs, institutions, and property, subject to constitutional limits.

## Official text

Subject to public order, morality and health, every religious denomination or any section thereof shall have the right to establish and maintain institutions for religious and charitable purposes, manage its own affairs in matters of religion, own and acquire property, and administer such property in accordance with law.

## Plain-English meaning

Religious groups do not only enjoy individual freedom of conscience under Article 25. Article 26 also protects collective religious autonomy, especially in matters of religious practice and institutional management, while allowing legal regulation of property and administration.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 26 is central when disputes arise over temple, mosque, church, mutt, or other denominational control and over the line between protected religious affairs and stateregulable secular administration.
How courts use it
Courts use the provision to ask whether a dispute concerns the core of religion, the rights of a denomination, or a secular administrative aspect that the state may regulate.
