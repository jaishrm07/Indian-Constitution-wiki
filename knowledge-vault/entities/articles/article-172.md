## Summary

Article 172 fixes the duration of State Legislatures, including the ordinary five-year life of Legislative Assemblies.

## Official text

Every Legislative Assembly of every State, unless sooner dissolved, shall continue for five years from the date appointed for its first meeting and no longer. The period may, while a Proclamation of Emergency is in operation, be extended by Parliament by law for a limited time.

## Plain-English meaning

State Assemblies normally last five years unless dissolved earlier. During an Emergency, Parliament may temporarily extend that period, but only within a tightly limited constitutional framework.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 172 is one of the core structural provisions behind debates over synchronized elections, premature dissolution, and the democratic cost of destabilizing an Assembly.
How courts and institutions use it
The provision matters when constitutional design questions turn on how long Assemblies may continue, when they end, and how election timing interacts with federal politics.
