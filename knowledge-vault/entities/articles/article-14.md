## Summary

Equality before the law and equal protection of the laws form the Constitution's baseline promise against arbitrary state action.

## Official text

The State shall not deny to any person equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India.

## Plain-English meaning

The state cannot treat similarly placed people arbitrarily. Courts use Article 14 to test whether executive and legislative action is reasoned, non-discriminatory, and constitutionally justified.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 14 is one of the Constitution's broadest guarantees. It operates both as an antiarbitrariness norm and as a discipline on state classification. Once the state distinguishes between groups, it needs a rational basis connected to a legitimate objective.
Reading the provision
The provision combines two ideas. Equality before law is a ruleoflaw promise. Equal protection of the laws asks whether people who are similarly situated are being treated in a constitutionally acceptable way.
How courts use it
Article 14 often appears with Articles 19 and 21 when courts review liberty restrictions, administrative discretion, welfare classifications, recruitment rules, or regulatory design.
