## Summary

Article 83 fixes the duration of the Houses of Parliament, including the ordinary five-year life of the Lok Sabha and the continuing character of the Rajya Sabha.

## Official text

The Council of States shall not be subject to dissolution, but as nearly as possible one-third of the members thereof shall retire as soon as may be on the expiration of every second year. The House of the People, unless sooner dissolved, shall continue for five years from the date appointed for its first meeting and no longer, subject to temporary extension during a Proclamation of Emergency.

## Plain-English meaning

The Rajya Sabha is a continuing chamber, while the Lok Sabha normally lasts five years unless dissolved earlier. During an Emergency, Parliament may temporarily extend the Lok Sabha's life by law, but only within constitutional limits.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 83 becomes central whenever debate turns to the life of the Lok Sabha, dissolution, synchronized election cycles, or the constitutional limits on extending representative mandates.
How courts and institutions use it
The provision matters less as everyday litigation text and more as a structural anchor for parliamentary tenure, electoral timing, and any proposal to redesign national election cycles.
