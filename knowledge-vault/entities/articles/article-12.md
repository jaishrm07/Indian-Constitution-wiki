## Summary

Article 12 defines what counts as the State for the purpose of enforcing fundamental rights.

## Official text

In this Part, unless the context otherwise requires, “the State” includes the Government and Parliament of India and the Government and the Legislature of each of the States and all local or other authorities within the territory of India or under the control of the Government of India.

## Plain-English meaning

Before rights can be enforced, courts need to know who is bound by them. Article 12 identifies the institutions and authorities whose actions can be tested against Part III.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Many constitutional disputes begin with a threshold question: is the body being challenged actually part of the State for Part III purposes. Article 12 provides that gateway.
How courts use it
The provision matters in cases involving public bodies, instrumentalities of government, and institutions that may not fit neatly inside traditional department structures.
