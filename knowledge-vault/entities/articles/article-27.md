## Summary

Article 27 protects people from being compelled to pay taxes specifically appropriated to promote or maintain a particular religion or denomination.

## Official text

No person shall be compelled to pay any taxes, the proceeds of which are specifically appropriated in payment of expenses for the promotion or maintenance of any particular religion or religious denomination.

## Plain-English meaning

The Constitution does not allow the state to force citizens to financially support the promotion or maintenance of a particular religion through specifically appropriated taxation.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 27 expresses one dimension of constitutional secularism in fiscal form. It matters whenever state support, taxation, or public finance is alleged to privilege a particular religion.
How courts and institutions use it
The provision becomes relevant when the argument is not only about religious freedom, but about whether state financing or taxation has crossed into constitutionally improper promotion of religion.
