## Summary

Article 32 guarantees the right to move the Supreme Court for enforcement of fundamental rights.

## Official text

The right to move the Supreme Court by appropriate proceedings for the enforcement of the rights conferred by this Part is guaranteed. The Supreme Court shall have power to issue directions or orders or writs, including writs in the nature of habeas corpus, mandamus, prohibition, quo warranto and certiorari, whichever may be appropriate, for the enforcement of any of the rights conferred by this Part.

## Plain-English meaning

This Article turns rights into enforceable claims. It gives the Supreme Court power to issue constitutional remedies such as writs when fundamental rights are violated.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Rights without remedies can become symbolic. Article 32 gives institutional force to Part III by allowing direct access to the Supreme Court for enforcement.
Reading the provision
The Article empowers the Court to issue writs and has long been described as part of the Constitution's remedial heart. It also reflects the centrality of judicial review in the constitutional design.
How courts use it
Public interest litigation, urgent liberty claims, and structural rights litigation often rely on Article 32, though the Court still exercises procedural discipline over when it will intervene directly.
