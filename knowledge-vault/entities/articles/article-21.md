## Summary

No person shall be deprived of life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law.

## Official text

No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law.

## Plain-English meaning

Article 21 began as a procedural protection, but the Supreme Court later expanded it into a broad guarantee of dignity, fairness, privacy, and substantive liberty.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 21 has become one of the most influential constitutional provisions in India. It supports doctrine on due process, prison conditions, legal aid, privacy, education, dignity, and state accountability.
Reading the provision
The text is short, but interpretation transformed it. The phrase procedure established by law is no longer read as allowing any formally valid procedure. Courts now ask whether that procedure is fair, just, and reasonable.
How courts use it
Modern Article 21 cases frequently interact with Article 14 and Article 19, especially when the state uses coercive power, surveillance, detention, or regulatory restrictions that affect individual dignity.
