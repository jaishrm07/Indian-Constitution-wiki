## Summary

Article 269A governs the levy and apportionment of goods and services tax on inter-State supplies.

## Official text

Goods and services tax on supplies in the course of inter-State trade or commerce shall be levied and collected by the Government of India and such tax shall be apportioned between the Union and the States in the manner as may be provided by Parliament by law on the recommendations of the Goods and Services Tax Council. Parliament may by law formulate the principles for determining the place of supply, and when a supply of goods, or of services, or both takes place in the course of inter-State trade or commerce.

## Plain-English meaning

This Article explains how inter-State GST is constitutionally collected by the Union and then shared with the states. It is a core provision for understanding how the GST system balances national coordination with state fiscal interests.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 269A shows how the Constitution can redesign revenue distribution for a shared national market. It is crucial for reading GST as a constitutional arrangement rather than a purely statutory tax reform.
How to read it
Read this Article with Article 246A and Article 279A. Those provisions together explain the power to tax, the sharing of interState revenue, and the institutional forum that steers the system.
