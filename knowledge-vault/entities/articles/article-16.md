## Summary

Article 16 guarantees equality of opportunity in public employment while also structuring reservation policy in state service.

## Official text

There shall be equality of opportunity for all citizens in matters relating to employment or appointment to any office under the State. No citizen shall, on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, descent, place of birth, residence or any of them, be ineligible for, or discriminated against in respect of, any employment or office under the State.

## Plain-English meaning

Public jobs cannot be distributed arbitrarily, but the Constitution also allows reservation and related measures within a carefully contested equality framework.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 16 sits at the centre of some of the most difficult equality debates in India because it combines antidiscrimination with the constitutional accommodation of reservation policy.
How courts use it
Courts often read Article 16 alongside Article 14 when assessing recruitment rules, promotional reservation, representational justice, and classification in public employment.
