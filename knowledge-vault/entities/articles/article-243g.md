## Summary

Article 243G empowers state legislatures to devolve powers and responsibilities to Panchayats as institutions of self-government.

## Official text

Subject to the provisions of this Constitution, the Legislature of a State may, by law, endow the Panchayats with such powers and authority as may be necessary to enable them to function as institutions of self-government and such law may contain provisions for the devolution of powers and responsibilities upon Panchayats at the appropriate level, subject to such conditions as may be specified therein, with respect to the preparation of plans for economic development and social justice and the implementation of schemes including those in relation to the matters listed in the Eleventh Schedule.

## Plain-English meaning

This Article is the operating heart of constitutional local government in rural India. It explains why Panchayats are not only administrative bodies but constitutional institutions that can receive real planning and implementation authority.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 243G is where decentralisation becomes a constitutional design question. It ties local democracy to planning, implementation, and functional devolution rather than leaving Panchayats to ordinary political preference.
How to read it
Read this Article with Part IX, the Eleventh Schedule, and the SeventyThird Amendment. Together they explain how rural selfgovernment is placed within the constitutional structure.
