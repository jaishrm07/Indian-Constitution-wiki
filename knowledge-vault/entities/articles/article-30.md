## Summary

Article 30 grants minorities the right to establish and administer educational institutions of their choice.

## Official text

All minorities, whether based on religion or language, shall have the right to establish and administer educational institutions of their choice. The State shall not, in granting aid to educational institutions, discriminate against any educational institution on the ground that it is under the management of a minority, whether based on religion or language.

## Plain-English meaning

Minority communities have a constitutionally protected role in setting up and running educational institutions, and the state cannot discriminate against such institutions when granting aid on the ground of minority management.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 30 is central to constitutional debates on educational autonomy, state regulation, and how pluralism is institutionally protected.
How courts use it
Courts often ask how far the state may regulate minority institutions without emptying the constitutional guarantee of meaningful administrative control.
