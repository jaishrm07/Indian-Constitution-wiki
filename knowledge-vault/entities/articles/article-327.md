## Summary

Article 327 empowers Parliament to legislate on elections to Parliament and State Legislatures, subject to the Constitution.

## Official text

Subject to the provisions of this Constitution, Parliament may from time to time by law make provision with respect to all matters relating to, or in connection with, elections to either House of Parliament or to the House or either House of the Legislature of a State including the preparation of electoral rolls, the delimitation of constituencies and all other matters necessary for securing the due constitution of such House or Houses.

## Plain-English meaning

The Constitution lets Parliament make detailed election law for legislative elections, including rolls, constituencies, and the machinery needed for properly constituting elected Houses.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 327 matters whenever electoral reform proposals move from constitutional theory to legislative design. It is the key bridge between constitutional election principles and the statutory machinery that implements them.
How courts and institutions use it
The provision is used to explain why Parliament can legislate broadly in the election field, while still remaining subject to constitutional limits and to the structure of Part XV.
