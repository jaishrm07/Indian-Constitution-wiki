## Summary

Article 201 governs what happens after a Governor reserves a State Bill for the consideration of the President.

## Official text

When a Bill is reserved by a Governor for the consideration of the President, the President shall declare either that he assents to the Bill or that he withholds assent therefrom. Where the Bill is not a Money Bill, the President may direct the Governor to return the Bill to the State Legislature for reconsideration before it is presented again for the President's consideration.

## Plain-English meaning

Once a Bill is reserved, the President may assent, withhold assent, or in some cases return the Bill through the Governor. Article 201 matters because reservation shifts a State legislative question into a Union-level constitutional channel.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 201 shows how a State Bill can migrate into a Union constitutional process. It therefore matters whenever reservation is used in politically contested state legislation.
How courts and institutions use it
The provision is important not only for formal assent mechanics, but also for understanding how reservation affects federal balance and the pace of democratic lawmaking.
