## Summary

Article 279A establishes the Goods and Services Tax Council and defines its recommendation-making role.

## Official text

The President shall, within sixty days from the date of commencement of the Constitution (One Hundred and First Amendment) Act, 2016, by order, constitute a Council to be called the Goods and Services Tax Council. The Goods and Services Tax Council shall make recommendations to the Union and the States on the taxes, cesses and surcharges to be subsumed, exemptions, model GST laws, apportionment principles under article 269A, rate structure, and other matters relating to the goods and services tax.

## Plain-English meaning

This Article creates the institutional table where the Union and states negotiate GST design. It is one of the clearest constitutional examples of cooperative federal decision-making.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 279A is where GST becomes a constitutional institution rather than only a tax. It turns fiscal federalism into an ongoing process of structured intergovernmental coordination.
How to read it
Read it with Articles 246A and 269A and the One Hundred and First Amendment. That trio explains the taxing power, the sharing rule, and the coordinating body that makes the system work.
