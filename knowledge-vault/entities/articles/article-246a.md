## Summary

Article 246A creates a special constitutional distribution of legislative power for goods and services tax.

## Official text

Notwithstanding anything contained in articles 246 and 254, Parliament, and, subject to clause (2), the Legislature of every State, have power to make laws with respect to goods and services tax imposed by the Union or by such State. Parliament has exclusive power to make laws with respect to goods and services tax where the supply of goods, or of services, or both takes place in the course of inter-State trade or commerce.

## Plain-English meaning

This Article is a major departure from the older list-based tax structure. It gives both Parliament and state legislatures constitutional power over GST, while reserving inter-State GST to Parliament.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 246A shows that fiscal federalism sometimes requires a constitutional redesign rather than a normal legislative adjustment. It is one of the clearest examples of constitutional innovation in contemporary India.
How to read it
Read it with Article 269A, Article 279A, the One Hundred and First Amendment, and the wider federalism debate. Together they explain how GST changed taxing power and intergovernmental coordination.
