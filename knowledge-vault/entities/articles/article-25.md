## Summary

Article 25 protects freedom of conscience and the free profession, practice, and propagation of religion, subject to constitutional limits.

## Official text

Subject to public order, morality and health and to the other provisions of this Part, all persons are equally entitled to freedom of conscience and the right freely to profess, practise and propagate religion. Nothing in this article shall affect the operation of any existing law or prevent the State from making any law regulating or restricting any economic, financial, political or other secular activity which may be associated with religious practice, or providing for social welfare and reform.

## Plain-English meaning

People have religious freedom, but it is not absolute. The Constitution allows the state to regulate secular activity linked to religion and to protect public order, health, and other constitutional values.

## Canonical site note

Why it matters
Article 25 stands at the intersection of liberty, equality, public order, and secular constitutional design. It is one of the main provisions through which the Constitution handles religious difference.
How courts use it
Courts often ask whether the dispute concerns protected religious practice, secular administration, or competing constitutional claims such as equality and dignity.
