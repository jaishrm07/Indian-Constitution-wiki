## Summary

Emergency provisions allow extraordinary state action, but their history makes constitutional safeguards especially important.

## Scope

Emergency powers need to be read with caution because the legal text creates exceptional tools with high stakes for federalism and rights.

## Canonical site note

Why this topic matters
Emergency provisions change the normal balance of constitutional power. They therefore require unusually careful attention to safeguards, judicial review, and historical misuse.
How to read the topic
Article 356 is the most visible entry point for contemporary readers because it raises recurring questions about Union intervention in state politics and the role of governors.
