## Summary

Parliamentary power in the Constitution covers law-making authority, amendment authority, and the institutional limits that prevent elected majorities from becoming constitutionally unlimited.

## Scope

This topic should be read through the tension between democratic mandate and constitutional restraint, especially where Parliament claims wide authority over foundational design.

## Canonical site note

Why this topic matters
Parliament is central to constitutional change, but its authority is not selfdefining. Questions about amendment power, procedure, and structural limits shape some of the most important constitutional conflicts in Indian public law.
How to read the topic
Start with the constitutional position of Parliament and then move to Article 368. The most important reading path is through amendmentera cases and the debate over whether constituent power can be broad without being unlimited.
