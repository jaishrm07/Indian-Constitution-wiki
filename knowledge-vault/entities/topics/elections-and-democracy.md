## Summary

Elections and democracy in the Constitution involve franchise, representation, periodic renewal of legislatures, and the institutional rules that preserve democratic legitimacy.

## Scope

This topic focuses on democratic participation itself, especially voting age, representation, and how electoral design affects constitutional legitimacy over time.

## Canonical site note

Why this topic matters
Some constitutional election questions are about institutional administration, but others are about democracy more directly: who votes, who gets represented, how constituencies are drawn, and when representative change actually takes effect.
How to read the topic
Read Articles 324 to 329 together with Article 326 and the amendment history around franchise, delimitation, and representation. Currentaffairs pages on delimitation and legislative reservation belong here because they change the democratic map, not just electoral procedure.
