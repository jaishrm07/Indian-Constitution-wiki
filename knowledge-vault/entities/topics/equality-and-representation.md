## Summary

Equality and representation bring together anti-discrimination doctrine, reservation design, and the constitutional effort to make representative institutions more inclusive.

## Scope

This topic sits where formal equality gives way to constitutional design choices about who is seen, counted, and represented in public institutions.

## Canonical site note

Why this topic matters
The Constitution does not treat equality as a purely abstract guarantee. It also uses representational devices, reservation, and tailored inclusion mechanisms to address structural disadvantage inside legislatures and public institutions.
How to read the topic
Move between Articles 14 to 16 and the representation provisions in Part XVI. Then read the amendment history that added or refined reservation design, especially when current disputes concern subclassification, legislative reservation, or the interaction between equality doctrine and democratic representation.
