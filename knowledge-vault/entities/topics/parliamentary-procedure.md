## Summary

Parliamentary procedure in constitutional law includes floor tests, defections, presiding-officer authority, and the rules that hold legislatures together under political stress.

## Scope

Procedure often looks technical until it determines who governs, who keeps office, and how legislative majorities are tested in practice.

## Canonical site note

Why this topic matters
Many constitutional crises are procedural before they are ideological. Antidefection disputes, recognition of party splits, the role of the Speaker, and the timing of floor tests can decide whether representative government remains stable or becomes manipulable.
How to read the topic
Read this topic through the Tenth Schedule, the powers of presiding officers, and the cases that decide when courts will intervene in legislative process. It often overlaps with federalism because statelevel instability becomes a constitutional problem quickly.
