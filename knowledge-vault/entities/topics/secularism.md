## Summary

Secularism in the Indian Constitution involves equal citizenship, religious freedom, and a state that cannot be reduced to majoritarian identity.

## Scope

Secularism is not only a political value; it is a constitutional principle that shapes rights, public institutions, and the limits of state action.
