## Summary

Free speech in India is constitutionally grounded in Article 19 and repeatedly tested by censorship, regulation, and platform governance.

## Scope

Free speech should be explained not as an abstract slogan but as a constitutional problem with textually limited restrictions and recurring modern forms.

## Canonical site note

Why this topic matters
Speech questions now arise across protest, media regulation, internet governance, hate speech, defamation, and intermediary liability. That makes free speech one of the Constitution's most publicly visible themes.
How to read the topic
Readers should begin with Article 19, then move to proportionality, procedural fairness, and the institutional role of courts in protecting public discourse.
