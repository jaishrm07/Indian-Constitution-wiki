## Summary

The Third Schedule contains the forms of oaths and affirmations for constitutional office-holders.

## Why it matters

This Schedule turns constitutional office into a formal commitment to the Constitution itself, not just to the institution or government of the day.

## Canonical site note

Why this Schedule matters
Oaths and affirmations are not ceremonial extras. They are the constitutional threshold for holding office and for accepting the duty to act within the Constitution's limits.
How to read it
Read this Schedule as the Constitution's language of fidelity. It sits behind ministers, legislators, judges, and other public officers by specifying the form in which they publicly commit to constitutional responsibility.
