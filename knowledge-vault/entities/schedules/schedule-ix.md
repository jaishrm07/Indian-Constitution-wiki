## Summary

The Ninth Schedule was created to protect specified laws from challenge, originally for land reform and later as a major test of constitutional limits.

## Why it matters

This Schedule became one of the Constitution's most contested devices because it tried to shield laws from judicial review while still operating inside the amendment power.

## Canonical site note

Why this Schedule matters
The Ninth Schedule sits at the centre of the relationship between reform legislation and constitutional review. It began as a protective device, but later became part of the basicstructure debate.
How to read it
Read it with Articles 13, 31A, 31B, and 368, then follow the case law that limits or explains how much protection the Schedule can actually provide. It is a schedule about insulation, but not complete immunity.
