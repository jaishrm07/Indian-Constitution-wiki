## Summary

The Sixth Schedule creates autonomous district and regional councils for selected tribal areas in the North-East.

## Why it matters

This Schedule is the Constitution's most detailed framework for local autonomy inside a state, balancing tribal self-government with Union and state structures.

## Canonical site note

Why this Schedule matters
The Sixth Schedule is a major constitutional autonomy design. It recognises that certain areas require selfgoverning institutions with powers tailored to local conditions and tribal identity.
How to read it
Read it with Article 244 and the special provisions for NorthEastern states. It is best understood as a constitutional compromise between territorial integrity and meaningful local autonomy.
