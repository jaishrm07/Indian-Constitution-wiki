## Summary

The Fifth Schedule provides a special constitutional framework for the administration of Scheduled Areas and the protection of Scheduled Tribes outside the Sixth Schedule system.

## Why it matters

This Schedule is one of the Constitution's clearest examples of asymmetric protection, designed to recognise vulnerability, land rights, and tribal governance within the federal structure.

## Canonical site note

Why this Schedule matters
The Fifth Schedule shows that the Constitution does not use one uniform administrative model everywhere. It creates a special framework where tribal protection and landbased governance need stronger constitutional attention.
How to read it
Read it alongside Articles 244, 339, and 46. The Schedule sits between welfare, territorial administration, and protective governance, making it central to constitutional discussions about tribal rights and state responsibility.
