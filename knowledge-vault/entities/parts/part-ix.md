## Summary

Part IX constitutionalises Panchayats and gives rural local self-government a formal place in the constitutional structure.

## Official role

This Part matters because it turns decentralisation into a constitutional commitment rather than a purely statutory choice.

## Reading path

- [[../articles/article-243g|Article 243G]]

## Canonical site note

Why this Part matters
Part IX shifts local democracy from the margins of governance into the constitutional text. It makes the structure of Panchayats a constitutional subject and ties rural devolution to democratic design.
How to read it
Readers should connect this Part with Article 243G, the Eleventh Schedule, and the SeventyThird Amendment. Together they explain how powers can move below the state level while still remaining constitutionally structured.
