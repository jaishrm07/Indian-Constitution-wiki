## Summary

Part IXA gives municipalities a constitutional footing and ties urban governance to decentralised democratic design.

## Official role

This Part matters because city government becomes a constitutional issue once municipalities are treated as institutions of self-government rather than mere administrative units.

## Reading path

- [[../articles/article-243w|Article 243W]]

## Canonical site note

Why this Part matters
Part IXA recognises that urban governance requires institutional design, representation, and devolution within the constitutional order. It is a major reason municipal structure can be debated in constitutional terms.
How to read it
Readers should connect this Part with Article 243W, the Twelfth Schedule, and the SeventyFourth Amendment. That cluster explains how urban selfgovernment is built into the constitutional map.
