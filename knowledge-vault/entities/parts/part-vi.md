## Summary

Part VI sets out the constitutional structure of States, including their legislatures, executives, and High Courts.

## Official role

This Part is central to state-level constitutional governance and to understanding why High Courts are such powerful constitutional forums.

## Reading path

- [[../articles/article-226|Article 226]]

## Canonical site note

Why this Part matters
Part VI shows that Indian constitutionalism is not only national in practice. State institutions, including High Courts, carry much of the Constitution's daytoday force.
How to read it
This Part should be read together with federalism, writ jurisdiction, and the constitutional relationship between Union and State institutions.
