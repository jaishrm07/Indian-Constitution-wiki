## Summary

Part VIII addresses the constitutional position of Union Territories, including special arrangements like the National Capital Territory of Delhi.

## Official role

This Part matters because Union Territories occupy a constitutional position that is neither identical to States nor reducible to ordinary administration.

## Canonical site note

Why this Part matters
The constitutional treatment of Union Territories reveals how India manages asymmetric governance arrangements inside a federal structure.
How to read it
Readers should use this Part when following disputes over Delhi, Lieutenant Governor powers, and the allocation of legislative or executive authority in Union Territories.
