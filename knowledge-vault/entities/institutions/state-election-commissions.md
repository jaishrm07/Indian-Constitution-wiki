## Summary

State Election Commissions are the constitutional authorities responsible for superintendence, direction, and control of local elections to panchayats and municipalities.

## Constitutional role

They schedule and supervise elections to local bodies and sit at the center of disputes over local election delay, ward revision, reservation rotation, and administrative interference.

## Why it matters

Local democracy depends on whether State Election Commissions can act as independent constitutional authorities rather than as administrative extensions of state governments.

## Constitutional basis

- Article 243K vests superintendence, direction, and control of panchayat elections in the State Election Commission.
- Article 243ZA extends the same constitutional logic to municipal elections.
