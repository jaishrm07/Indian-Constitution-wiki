## Summary

State governments are central to federal conflict, reservation policy, legislative standoffs, and many of the constitutional controversies that play out below the Union level.

## Constitutional role

State governments exercise executive power within their constitutional sphere and often become the primary actors in disputes over federal autonomy, public policy, and political legitimacy.

## Why it matters

A Constitution-focused site cannot explain Indian public law well if it treats all constitutional conflict as Union-level conflict.

## Constitutional basis

- Part VI structures state executives and their relation to legislatures.
- Part XI matters where state power overlaps with Union competence.
