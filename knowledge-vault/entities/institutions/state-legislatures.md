## Summary

State legislatures are where federal accountability, floor tests, reservation design, and state-level constitutional politics become institutionally real.

## Constitutional role

State legislatures make state law, test legislative majority, and become the arena for many constitutional disputes over government formation, representation, and federal balance.

## Why it matters

Many constitutional current-affairs issues are really disputes about how state legislatures function, who controls them, and whether they are allowed to remain the primary democratic forum.

## Constitutional basis

- Part VI structures state government and legislatures.
- Article 356 matters when the ordinary functioning of a state legislature is said to have broken down.
