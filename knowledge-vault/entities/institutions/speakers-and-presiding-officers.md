## Summary

Speakers and presiding officers matter most when legislative procedure, anti-defection disputes, and government survival depend on timing and control of the House.

## Constitutional role

These officers control important aspects of legislative procedure and can become constitutionally significant when floor tests, disqualifications, or agenda-setting alter political outcomes.

## Why it matters

Anti-defection disputes and floor-test controversies often turn less on abstract law and more on how much procedural power a presiding officer can wield at a politically decisive moment.

## Constitutional basis

- Articles 93 and 178 create the office of Speaker in Parliament and state legislatures.
- The Tenth Schedule matters when presiding officers decide anti-defection disputes.
