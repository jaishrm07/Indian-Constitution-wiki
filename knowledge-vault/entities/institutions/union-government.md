## Summary

The Union government is the main executive actor behind national legislation, policy design, institutional reform proposals, and many of the constitutional disputes that reach the courts.

## Constitutional role

The Union government initiates legislation, defends executive action, proposes structural reform, and shapes the constitutional debate through policy design and litigation positions.

## Why it matters

Many constitutional questions cannot be understood only as abstract legal disputes; they are also about what the Union executive is trying to do through law, regulation, or institutional redesign.

## Constitutional basis

- Part V structures the Union executive and its relationship with constitutional offices.
- Part XI and Part XX matter when the Union seeks to reshape federal or constitutional arrangements.
