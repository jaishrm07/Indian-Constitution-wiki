## Summary

The Department of Justice is the Union executive node through which judicial appointments, transfers, and related notifications are processed and published.

## Constitutional role

The Department turns recommendations and constitutional appointments into executive notifications, tracks transfer and appointment orders, and provides the government's public-facing appointments record.

## Why it matters

Judicial appointments are not visible only through judgments or collegium resolutions. The executive processing layer matters because recommendations, delays, reiterations, and warrants all pass through this institutional channel.

## Constitutional basis

- Articles 124, 217, and 222 provide the constitutional framework within which appointments and transfers are processed.
- The Department is not itself a constitutional office, but it is the Union executive interface through which appointment and transfer decisions become official notifications.
