## Summary

The President is the formal head of the Union and appears in constitutional questions involving assent, ordinances, emergency proclamations, and formal constitutional action.

## Constitutional role

The President acts within the constitutional structure of the Union executive and appears as the formal constitutional authority behind assent, proclamations, and other major state acts.

## Why it matters

Readers often need to distinguish between formal constitutional action in the President's name and the underlying political or cabinet process that drives it.

## Constitutional basis

- Articles 52 to 78 structure the Union executive and the office of the President.
- Article 356 and emergency provisions matter when the President issues proclamations affecting state government.
