## Summary

Governors are state-level constitutional functionaries whose role becomes especially contested in hung assemblies, assent disputes, and centre-state friction.

## Constitutional role

Governors act within the constitutional framework of state government, but disputes often arise over discretion, floor tests, assent, and the relationship between elected governments and constitutional offices.

## Why it matters

Governor-related controversies are one of the clearest examples of constitutional design being tested by real political conflict.

## Constitutional basis

- Articles 153 to 162 structure the office of the Governor and its relation to the state executive.
- Article 356 becomes relevant when disputes escalate into claims that constitutional machinery has broken down.
