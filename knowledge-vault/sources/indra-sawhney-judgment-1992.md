## What this source is

Official Supreme Court judgment upholding OBC reservations under the Mandal Commission recommendations, establishing that reservations cannot exceed 50% of available seats, mandating the exclusion of the creamy layer, and holding that reservations in promotions require independent constitutional justification.

## Why it matters

Use for OBC reservations and the 50% ceiling, creamy layer exclusion from reservations, reservations in promotions under Article 16, backward class identification methodology.

## Key claims or extracts

- OBC reservations and the 50% ceiling
- creamy layer exclusion from reservations
- reservations in promotions under Article 16
- backward class identification methodology

## Limits and caveats

Nine-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 16 November 1992. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation AIR 1993 SC 477. The most-cited portions are the majority opinion on the 50% ceiling, the separate holdings on the creamy layer, and the discussion of what constitutes a "backward class" under Article 16(4). Justice Jeevan Reddy wrote the leading judgment; six separate opinions were delivered.
