## What this source is

Official Supreme Court judgment transforming the interpretation of Article 21 by holding that any procedure established by law must satisfy standards of reasonableness, fairness, and non-arbitrariness, and that Articles 14, 19, and 21 form an interdependent triad of fundamental rights.

## Why it matters

Use for Article 21 right to life and personal liberty, reasonableness and fairness of procedure established by law, Articles 14, 19, and 21 as a mutually reinforcing triad, passport impoundment and freedom to travel abroad.

## Key claims or extracts

- Article 21 right to life and personal liberty
- reasonableness and fairness of procedure established by law
- Articles 14, 19, and 21 as a mutually reinforcing triad
- passport impoundment and freedom to travel abroad

## Limits and caveats

Seven-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 25 January 1978. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation AIR 1978 SC 597. The most-cited portions are Justice Bhagwati's majority opinion expanding Article 21 beyond the narrow reading in A.K. Gopalan, the discussion of the interrelationship between Articles 14, 19, and 21, and the holding that any procedure depriving personal liberty must be reasonable, fair, and just.
