## What this source is

Unanimous five-judge Constitution Bench judgment holding that the Lieutenant Governor of Delhi must act on the aid and advice of the elected Council of Ministers in all matters within the Delhi Legislative Assembly's competence. The ruling reinforced the constitutional primacy of the elected executive in Delhi while carving out the three areas — police, public order, and land — over which the Union retains direct control under Article 239AA.

## Why it matters

Use for Article 239AA and the constitutional status of Delhi, Lieutenant Governor's role and limits on discretionary power, aid and advice of elected Council of Ministers binding on LG, democratic accountability and cooperative federalism in Union Territories, exceptions for police, public order, and land in Delhi's legislative competence.

## Limits and caveats

Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 4 July 2018 (unanimous). Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (2018) 8 SCC 501. The bench (Dipak Misra CJ, A.K. Sikri, A.M. Khanwilkar, D.Y. Chandrachud, and Ashok Bhushan JJ) construed Article 239AA and held that the Lieutenant Governor of Delhi is bound by the aid and advice of the elected Council of Ministers on matters falling within the legislative competence of the Delhi Legislative Assembly, and has no independent decision-making power in such matters. The LG's discretionary power is limited to matters reserved for the President under Article 239AA(4) and the three subjects — police, public order, and land — over which Parliament retains exclusive competence. The majority emphasised the democratic purpose of Article 239AA and directed the LG and the Delhi government to work in a spirit of collaborative governance. Specific service-matters questions were subsequently referred to a separate Constitution Bench (see delhi-services-judgment-2023).
