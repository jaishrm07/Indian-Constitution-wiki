## What this source is

Seven-judge Constitution Bench judgment overruling the 1967 decision in S. Azeez Basha and holding that Aligarh Muslim University can in principle qualify as a minority institution protected under Article 30, even though it was incorporated by an Act of Parliament. The court articulated a three-part test for minority status and remanded the question of AMU's actual minority character to a regular bench for factual determination.

## Limits and caveats

Seven-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 8 November 2024. Retrieve via eSCR portal. The bench (4:3) overruled the 1967 five-judge bench decision in S. Azeez Basha v. Union of India, which had held that AMU, being incorporated under a central statute, could not claim minority status under Article 30. The majority laid down a three-part test for minority status — (1) whether the community is a minority, (2) whether the institution was established by that minority, and (3) whether it was established for the education of that minority. The majority held that the mere fact that an institution derives its legal existence from a statute does not strip it of minority character if the founding initiative belonged to the minority community. The factual question of whether AMU satisfies the test was remanded to a regular bench for fresh determination.
