## What this source is

Official Supreme Court judgment curtailing misuse of Article 356 by holding that Presidential satisfaction for imposing President's Rule is subject to judicial review, that a floor test is the appropriate means of testing a government's majority, and that federalism is an inviolable basic structure feature.

## Why it matters

Use for Article 356 President's Rule and its limits, judicial review of Presidential satisfaction, floor test as the preferred mechanism for testing majority, federalism as a basic structure feature.

## Key claims or extracts

- Article 356 President's Rule and its limits
- judicial review of Presidential satisfaction
- floor test as the preferred mechanism for testing majority
- federalism as a basic structure feature

## Limits and caveats

Nine-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 11 March 1994. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation AIR 1994 SC 1918. The most-cited portions are the holdings on judicial reviewability of Presidential satisfaction, the requirement of a floor test before dismissal, and the multiple concurring opinions affirming federalism as part of the basic structure of the Constitution.
