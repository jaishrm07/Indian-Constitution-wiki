## What this source is

Official Supreme Court judgment reading down Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code to decriminalise consensual same-sex conduct among adults, holding that criminalisation violated Articles 14, 15, 19, and 21, and establishing that constitutional morality must prevail over majoritarian social morality.

## Why it matters

Use for decriminalisation of consensual same-sex relations, Section 377 IPC and constitutional validity, constitutional morality versus social morality, sexual orientation and Articles 14, 15, 19, and 21, dignity and autonomy under fundamental rights.

## Key claims or extracts

- decriminalisation of consensual same-sex relations
- Section 377 IPC and constitutional validity
- constitutional morality versus social morality
- sexual orientation and Articles 14, 15, 19, and 21
- dignity and autonomy under fundamental rights

## Limits and caveats

Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 6 September 2018. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation AIR 2018 SC 4321. Four separate concurring opinions were delivered by CJI Dipak Misra, Justice Rohinton Nariman, Justice D.Y. Chandrachud, and Justice Indu Malhotra. The most-cited portions address constitutional morality, the right to identity, and the reading down of Section 377 to exclude consensual same-sex relations between adults.
