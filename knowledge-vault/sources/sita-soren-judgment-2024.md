## What this source is

Official Supreme Court judgment clarifying that parliamentary privilege does not shield legislators from bribery prosecution, overruling the 1998 P.V. Narasimha Rao majority on this point.

## Why it matters

Use for legislative privilege under Articles 105 and 194, bribery prosecution of legislators, Tenth Schedule and corruption.

## Key claims or extracts

- legislative privilege under Articles 105 and 194
- bribery prosecution of legislators
- Tenth Schedule and corruption

## Limits and caveats

Seven-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 4 March 2024 (unanimous). Retrieve via eSCR portal. Overruled the majority in P.V. Narasimha Rao v. State (1998) and held that Articles 105(2) and 194(2) do not protect legislators from prosecution for bribery antecedent to a speech or vote.
