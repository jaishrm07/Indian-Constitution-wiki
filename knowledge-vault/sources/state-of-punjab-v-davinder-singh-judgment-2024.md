## What this source is

Official Supreme Court judgment establishing that States may sub-classify within Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe reservation categories to direct benefits to the most disadvantaged, subject to empirical justification.

## Why it matters

Use for sub-classification within SC/ST categories, Article 14 and Article 16 reservation law, Scheduled Caste reservation design.

## Key claims or extracts

- sub-classification within SC/ST categories
- Article 14 and Article 16 reservation law
- Scheduled Caste reservation design

## Limits and caveats

Seven-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 1 August 2024 (6:1 majority). Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (2024) 9 SCC 431. Overruled E.V. Chinnaiah (2005) and held that sub-classification within SC/ST reservation categories is constitutionally permissible.
