## What this source is

Official Supreme Court judgment in which a Constitution Bench issued eleven binding procedural guidelines — the DK Basu Guidelines — governing arrest and detention to prevent custodial torture and deaths, holding that breach of these guidelines constitutes contempt of court and that monetary compensation under Article 32 is a constitutionally available remedy for custodial violations of Article 21.

## Limits and caveats

Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 18 December 1996, reported in 1997. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (1997) 1 SCC 416 or AIR 1997 SC 610. The Court was moved by a letter-petition highlighting custodial deaths and torture. The bench laid down eleven binding requirements — collectively known as the DK Basu Guidelines — governing arrest, detention, and interrogation: arresting officers must bear accurate, visible name-tags; a memo of arrest must be prepared and witnessed; the arrested person must be informed of the right to have a relative notified; medical examination at the time of arrest and periodically during custody; the arrested person must be made aware of the right to consult a lawyer; and a district-level Control Room must maintain a register of arrestees. Failure to comply constitutes contempt of court. The Court also affirmed that monetary compensation is an appropriate remedy under Article 32 for established custodial violations of Article 21.
