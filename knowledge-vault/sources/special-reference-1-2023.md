## What this source is

Official Supreme Court judgment in which a five-judge Constitution Bench unanimously upheld the 2019 abrogation of Article 370 and the bifurcation of Jammu & Kashmir into two Union Territories, holding that Article 370 was a temporary provision subject to Presidential abrogation, and directing restoration of J&K's statehood without fixing a deadline.

## Limits and caveats

Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 11 December 2023. Retrieve via eSCR portal; citation reported as (2024) 1 SCC 1. The bench unanimously upheld the validity of Presidential Order C.O. 272 (5 August 2019), which applied all provisions of the Constitution to Jammu & Kashmir, and C.O. 273, which rendered Article 370 inoperative by substituting a new clause (3) that permitted abrogation on Presidential recommendation alone. The Court held that Article 370 was a temporary provision intended to facilitate the gradual integration of J&K with the Union and that it could be rendered inoperative by the President acting on the concurrence of Parliament (standing in for the J&K Constituent Assembly, which had ceased to exist). The bifurcation of J&K into the Union Territories of J&K and Ladakh under the Jammu and Kashmir Reorganisation Act 2019 was also upheld under Article 3. The bench directed the Election Commission of India to conduct elections to the J&K Legislative Assembly by 30 September 2024 and held that statehood must be restored to J&K, though no specific timeline was set for that restoration.
