# What this source is

The official Supreme Court judgment that treats prolonged gubernatorial inaction on bills as a constitutional problem.

## Why it matters

This is the primary source for the Court's reasoning on Article 200, delay, reservation, and the limits of executive inaction over state legislation.

## Key claims or extracts

- the Governor cannot use silence as an unlimited veto
- Article 200 must be read as a constitutional choice, not open-ended inaction
- Article 201 cannot be expanded into a general delay device
