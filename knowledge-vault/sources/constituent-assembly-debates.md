## What this source is

The official record of the Constituent Assembly Debates (1946-1949), published by the Parliament of India / Lok Sabha Secretariat, comprising twelve volumes that document the deliberations of the framers and constitute the primary source for understanding the intent, purpose, and original meaning of provisions of the Constitution of India.

## Why it matters

Use for intent behind constitutional provisions, drafting history and original understanding of fundamental rights, framers' debates on federalism, parliamentary democracy, and judicial review, background to specific articles and schedules of the Constitution, interpretation of ambiguous constitutional text through historical context.

## Key claims or extracts

## Limits and caveats

The Constituent Assembly met from 9 December 1946 to 26 November 1949 over 11 sessions. The full debates — comprising 12 volumes — are digitised and available through the Parliament Digital Library at eparlib.sansad.in as well as through the parliamentofindia.nic.in portal. They are the primary source for understanding the intent behind constitutional provisions. Searches by article number, speaker name, or keyword are supported on the Parliament Digital Library portal. The debates are frequently cited by the Supreme Court in constitutional interpretation, including landmark judgments such as Kesavananda Bharati (1973), Maneka Gandhi (1978), and Indra Sawhney (1992).
