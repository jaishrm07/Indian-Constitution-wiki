## What this source is

Official Supreme Court judgment in which a five-judge bench held that the right to livelihood is an integral component of the right to life under Article 21, that any procedure depriving a person of their livelihood must be just, fair, and reasonable, and that municipal authorities must give prior notice and a hearing before evicting pavement and slum dwellers.

## Why it matters

Use for right to livelihood as part of Article 21, procedural fairness before eviction of pavement and slum dwellers, procedure established by law under Article 21 and the just, fair, and reasonable test, expansive reading of the right to life.

## Limits and caveats

Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 10 July 1985. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (1985) 3 SCC 545 or AIR 1986 SC 180. The petitioners were pavement and slum dwellers challenging Bombay Municipal Corporation notices seeking to evict them. The Court held that the right to life under Article 21 encompasses the right to livelihood, because no person can live without the means of living. Deprivation of livelihood therefore amounts to deprivation of life within the meaning of Article 21, and such deprivation must be carried out only by procedure that is just, fair, and reasonable. The Court further held that before evicting pavement dwellers the authorities must give prior notice and an opportunity to be heard, and directed that evictions be deferred until the end of the monsoon season.
