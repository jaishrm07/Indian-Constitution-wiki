## The constitutional frame

Reservation design is not a departure from equality but part of one constitutional approach to equality. The harder disputes concern category design, internal differentiation, and the justification for specific state choices.

## What readers should watch

The key issues are classification logic, evidentiary basis, representational claims, and whether the framework remains consistent with constitutional structure.

## Chronology

- **August 2020**: A five-judge Constitution Bench referred the sub-classification question arising in E.V. Chinnaiah v. State of Andhra Pradesh to a larger bench, acknowledging that the earlier ruling required reconsideration.
- **October 2022**: A seven-judge Constitution Bench was constituted specifically to examine whether State governments can sub-classify within Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe reservation categories to prioritise the more backward among them.
- **August 2024**: The seven-judge bench in State of Punjab v. Davinder Singh held 6:1 that sub-classification within SC/ST categories is constitutionally permissible; States may create preference tiers to direct reservation benefits to the most backward sub-groups within a reserved category.
- **October 2024**: Directions for implementation were issued by the Court; State governments began reviewing existing reservation policies and initiating studies to justify sub-classification schemes on an evidentiary basis.
- **2025 onwards**: Multiple State governments proceeded with sub-classification schemes; legal challenges were filed in various High Courts questioning the criteria used and whether States had adequate empirical basis for differentiating within SC/ST categories.

## What is constitutionally at stake

Sub-classification raises the question whether the homogeneity assumption in reservation law — that all members of a Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe list are equally placed — is constitutionally accurate, or whether differential treatment within the category is not only permissible but required by Article 14's substantive equality guarantee. The Davinder Singh majority held that sub-classification is not a sub-division that creates a new class forbidden by the Constitution, but a mechanism that directs the benefit of reservation to those who need it most, consistent with the purpose for which Articles 15(4) and 16(4) were enacted. The dissent warned that fragmenting the SC/ST categories risks undermining the political solidarity and collective identity on which scheduled-caste and scheduled-tribe representation in legislatures and public services rests. The live constitutional question is now implementation: what criteria States may legitimately use to draw internal tiers, whether sub-classification can be applied to promotions under Article 16(4A), and whether the creamy layer concept developed for Other Backward Classes in Indra Sawhney will now be imported into SC/ST law despite earlier Supreme Court decisions holding it inapplicable to those categories. The answers will determine the practical reach of reservation for the most disadvantaged sub-groups within each list, and whether the judiciary treats empirical justification as a constitutional prerequisite or merely a best-practice expectation.

## How to follow this issue

Monitor State government notifications and gazette orders implementing sub-classification schemes, petitions and judgments in relevant High Courts challenging those schemes, and any further Supreme Court orders specifying implementation parameters or granting or refusing stays. The Supreme Court of India's case-status portal and the PRS Legislative Research website are the most reliable publicly accessible sources for tracking both judicial proceedings and legislative or executive action on this issue. Watch in particular for whether the Court issues guidelines on what empirical material States must produce to justify internal categorisation, and whether any State's scheme is struck down for insufficient evidentiary basis.

- **Supreme Court of India** (`supreme-court-of-india`): Daily orders and cause lists for ongoing sub-classification implementation challenges.
- **PRS Legislative Research** (`prs-india`): Summaries of State reservation notifications and constitutional amendment history.
