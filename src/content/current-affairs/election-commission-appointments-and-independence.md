## The constitutional frame

Article 324 does more than create an administrative body. It creates a constitutional election authority whose credibility depends on institutional design, not only on formal powers.

## What readers should watch

The most important question is whether independence is protected only on paper or actually built into the appointments and tenure structure.

## Chronology

- **2023:** Anoop Baranwal v. Union of India — a five-judge Constitution Bench holds that the existing executive-only appointment process for Election Commissioners violates constitutional requirements; directs an interim collegium of the Prime Minister, Leader of Opposition, and Chief Justice of India to make appointments until Parliament legislates.
- **August 2023:** Chief Election Commissioner and Other Election Commissioners (Appointment, Conditions of Service and Term of Office) Act, 2023 introduced; the Bill replaces the Chief Justice of India on the selection committee with a Union Cabinet Minister nominated by the Prime Minister.
- **December 2023:** The Act passes both Houses of Parliament and receives Presidential assent on 28 December 2023; brought into force with effect from 2 January 2024.
- **October 2023:** First appointments under the new statutory framework are made using the reconstituted selection panel.
- **March 2024:** Gyanesh Kumar and Sukhbir Singh Sandhu appointed as Election Commissioners with effect from 15 March 2024 under the new law.
- **2024:** Opposition and civil-society petitioners challenge the 2023 Act in the Supreme Court, arguing it restores executive dominance and violates the structural principle laid down in Anoop Baranwal.
- **February 2025:** Gazette notifications dated 17 February 2025 name Gyanesh Kumar as Chief Election Commissioner and Dr. Vivek Joshi as Election Commissioner; both assume charge under the new law on 19 February 2025.
- **2025:** Supreme Court proceedings on the constitutional challenge to the 2023 Act continue; the question of whether Parliament's chosen model satisfies Article 324 remains live.

## What is constitutionally at stake

Article 324 vests superintendence, direction, and control of elections in the Election Commission of India. The constitutional text specifies that the Chief Election Commissioner can be removed only by the same process as a Supreme Court judge, but leaves Parliament discretion to legislate on the appointment process. The Anoop Baranwal judgment held that an all-executive appointment process was constitutionally inadequate because institutional independence is essential to Article 324's function. The 2023 Act replaced judicial involvement with a Cabinet Minister nominated by the Prime Minister, which petitioners argue recreates the structural defect the Court identified. The live constitutional question is whether Parliament's choice of appointment mechanism is committed to its legislative discretion or whether the institutional-independence guarantee under Article 324 imposes a justiciable constraint on that choice.

## How to follow this issue

Monitor Supreme Court proceedings on the challenge to the 2023 Act, including any interim orders on appointments made under the contested framework. Track new Election Commissioner appointments and the composition of selection-committee meetings for evidence of how the statutory panel operates in practice. Consult Election Commission annual reports and press releases on electoral administration, and watch for any parliamentary bills proposing further amendments to the appointments process.
