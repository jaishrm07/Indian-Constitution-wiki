## The constitutional frame

The constitutional change is already on the books, but the operational question is timing. The amendment ties commencement to a future delimitation exercise after the relevant census figures are published, so the live issue is how Parliament, the Election Commission, and state institutions translate the text into seat allocation.

## What readers should watch

The important signals are whether enabling law appears, how Delhi is handled, and whether the rotation of reserved seats preserves the one-third design without creating avoidable constitutional friction.

## Chronology

- **19 September 2023**: The Constitution (One Hundred and Sixth Amendment) Bill, 2023 was introduced in the Lok Sabha; it was passed by the Lok Sabha on 20 September 2023 and by the Rajya Sabha on 21 September 2023.
- **28 September 2023**: The Constitution (One Hundred and Sixth Amendment) Act, 2023 received presidential assent, inserting Articles 330A, 332A, and 334A to reserve one-third of seats in the House of the People, State Legislative Assemblies, and the Legislative Assembly of the National Capital Territory of Delhi for women.
- **Commencement condition**: The Act expressly provides that the reservation takes effect only after the next delimitation exercise conducted on the basis of census figures published after the commencement of the Act — meaning the right exists in the constitutional text but cannot be exercised until that delimitation is complete.
- **2021 census (postponed)**: The decennial census due in 2021 was deferred because of the COVID-19 pandemic and as of early 2026 has not been conducted, pushing back the entire sequence of census, delimitation, and commencement of women's reservation.
- **2026**: The constitutional freeze on seat-count delimitation (extended by the 84th Amendment until 2026) lapses, creating the earliest legal window for fresh delimitation, but the absent census data remains the practical bottleneck.
- **One Nation One Election referral**: The Joint Parliamentary Committee examining simultaneous elections has intersecting implications for delimitation timing, since any restructuring of the electoral calendar would affect when and how the delimitation triggering women's reservation could be carried out.

## What is constitutionally at stake

The 106th Amendment creates a constitutional right with a deferred and condition-dependent trigger: the reservation exists on the face of the Constitution but cannot be exercised until a delimitation exercise is conducted, and that delimitation cannot legally proceed until census data is published. This architecture creates a structural gap between constitutional promise and political reality, and the longer the census is delayed, the further the effective commencement of women's reservation recedes. The central constitutional tension is whether Parliament's choice to condition commencement on a future administrative and legislative act — delimitation — is a permissible staging mechanism or whether indefinite deferral of the commencement date effectively nullifies the amendment's intent and could, in an extreme case, be argued to touch the basic structure of the Constitution as it relates to representation and equality. A secondary tension arises from the rotation mechanism for reserved seats: if reserved constituencies rotate after each general election, the design must avoid inadvertently concentrating or dispersing women's representation in ways that undermine the one-third floor. Any litigation over commencement timing or rotation rules will require courts to consider how constitutional amendments interact with procedural preconditions Parliament itself has written in.

## How to follow this issue

Monitor official announcements of census commencement and completion from the Office of the Registrar General and Census Commissioner of India, and watch for the Gazette notification constituting a Delimitation Commission under the Delimitation Act once census figures are published. Parliamentary debates and Question Hour responses on whether the commencement date should be decoupled from delimitation — allowing women's reservation to commence on a fixed date regardless of boundary changes — are the most direct signal of political intent. The Election Commission of India's press releases and the Ministry of Law and Justice's legislative department portal are the official sources for delimitation and electoral law developments; PRS Legislative Research provides accessible summaries of parliamentary proceedings on this issue.
