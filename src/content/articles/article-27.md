## Why it matters

Article 27 expresses one dimension of constitutional secularism in fiscal form. It matters whenever state support, taxation, or public finance is alleged to privilege a particular religion.

## How courts and institutions use it

The provision becomes relevant when the argument is not only about religious freedom, but about whether state financing or taxation has crossed into constitutionally improper promotion of religion.
