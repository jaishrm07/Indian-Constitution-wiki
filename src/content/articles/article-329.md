  329. Bar to interference by courts in electoral matters.— [Notwithstanding anything in this Constitution —]

  (a) the validity of any law relating to the delimitation of constituencies or the allotment of seats to such constituencies, made or purporting to be made under article 327 or article 328, shall not be called in question in any court;

  (b) no election to either House of Parliament or to the House or either House of the Legislature of a State shall be called in question except by an election petition presented to such authority and in such manner as may be provided for by or under any law made by the appropriate Legislature.

## Why it matters

Article 329 is one of the Constitution's clearest attempts to protect an institutional process from constant interruption. It shapes when courts step in and when they wait.

## How courts use it

Courts use Article 329 to distinguish between pre-election intervention and post-election challenge, and to preserve the integrity and continuity of the electoral process.
