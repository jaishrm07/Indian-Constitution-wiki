  assents to the Bill or that he withholds assent therefrom or that he reserves

  in certain cases to return a non-Money Bill for reconsideration.

## Why it matters

Article 200 is where ordinary state lawmaking can become a constitutional flashpoint. Assent disputes raise questions about federal balance, democratic accountability, and the permissible use of constitutional offices.

## How courts and institutions use it

The provision matters whenever the argument shifts from legislative disagreement to constitutional obstruction, reservation of Bills, or delay in completing the lawmaking process.
