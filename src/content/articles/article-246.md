  enumerated in List I in the Seventh Schedule. Parliament, and, subject to
  clause (1), the Legislature of any State also, have power to make laws with
  respect to any of the matters enumerated in List III in the Seventh Schedule.
  Subject to clauses (1) and (2), the Legislature of any State has exclusive
  power to make laws for such State or any part thereof with respect to any of
  the matters enumerated in List II in the Seventh Schedule.

## Why it matters

Legislative competence disputes usually begin here. Article 246 is where constitutional structure translates into practical questions about taxation, regulation, administration, and overlap between different levels of government.

## How courts use it

Courts read Article 246 together with the Seventh Schedule to resolve conflicts over legislative fields, repugnancy arguments, and the constitutional reach of central legislation.
