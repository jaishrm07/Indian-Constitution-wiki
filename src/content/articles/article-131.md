## Why it matters

Article 131 is one of the Constitution's most important federal dispute mechanisms. It shows that constitutional structure requires a direct forum for certain inter-governmental conflicts.

## How courts use it

The provision matters when the Union and States clash over powers, legal rights, institutional obligations, or the constitutional validity of specific arrangements.
