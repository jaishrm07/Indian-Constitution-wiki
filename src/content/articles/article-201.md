  201. Bills reserved for consideration.—When a Bill is reserved by a Governor for the consideration of the President, the President shall declare either that he assents to the Bill or that he withholds assent therefrom:

  Provided that, where the Bill is not a Money Bill, the President may direct the Governor to return the Bill to the House or, as the case may be, the Houses of the Legislature of the State together with such a message as is mentioned in the first proviso to article 200 and, when a Bill is so returned, the House or Houses shall reconsider it accordingly within a period of six months from the date of receipt of such message and, if it is again passed by the House or Houses with or without amendment, it shall be presented again to the President for his consideration. ## Why it matters

Article 201 shows how a State Bill can migrate into a Union constitutional process. It therefore matters whenever reservation is used in politically contested state legislation.

## How courts and institutions use it

The provision is important not only for formal assent mechanics, but also for understanding how reservation affects federal balance and the pace of democratic lawmaking.
