## Why it matters

Article 14 is one of the Constitution's broadest guarantees. It operates both as an anti-arbitrariness norm and as a discipline on state classification. Once the state distinguishes between groups, it needs a rational basis connected to a legitimate objective.

## Reading the provision

The provision combines two ideas. Equality before law is a rule-of-law promise. Equal protection of the laws asks whether people who are similarly situated are being treated in a constitutionally acceptable way.

## How courts use it

Article 14 often appears with Articles 19 and 21 when courts review liberty restrictions, administrative discretion, welfare classifications, recruitment rules, or regulatory design.
