## Why it matters

Article 356 lies at the fault line between Union power and state autonomy. Its history shows why constitutional text alone is not enough without judicial limits and political restraint.

## Reading the provision

The formal trigger is a failure of constitutional machinery in a state. In practice, that phrase can be stretched, which is why later case law became crucial to protect federalism.

## How courts use it

The Supreme Court's scrutiny of Article 356 proclamations focuses on material, mala fides, and whether legislative majority should instead be tested on the floor of the assembly.
