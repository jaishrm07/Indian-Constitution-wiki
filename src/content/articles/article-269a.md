  Goods and services tax on supplies in the course of inter-State trade or
  commerce shall be levied and collected by the Government of India and such
  tax shall be apportioned between the Union and the States in the manner as
  may be provided by Parliament by law on the recommendations of the Goods

  determining the place of supply, and when a supply of goods, or of services,

## Why it matters

Article 269A shows how the Constitution can redesign revenue distribution for a shared national market. It is crucial for reading GST as a constitutional arrangement rather than a purely statutory tax reform.

## How to read it

Read this Article with Article 246A and Article 279A. Those provisions together explain the power to tax, the sharing of inter-State revenue, and the institutional forum that steers the system.
