## Why the case remains central

This case matters because it refused to let the state defend constitutional action by choosing the right label. The Court asked what the law actually did to rights holders.

## Doctrinal line

That effect-based approach became one of the foundations for later fairness and dignity reasoning, including the wider turn in Maneka Gandhi.

## Long-term impact

The case remains important in economic-rights debates, compensation disputes, and any argument where state power is defended through formal wording rather than practical consequences.
