## Why the case remains central

The Court drew a hard line between privilege and corruption. A legislator cannot treat privilege as a shield for taking a bribe, even if the bribe is linked to a promised speech or vote.

## Long-term impact

The case is now the starting point for any discussion of legislative immunity, corruption, and the limits of P.V. Narasimha Rao. It also clarifies that the criminal law analysis turns on the completed offence of bribery, not on whether the promised act was ultimately performed.
