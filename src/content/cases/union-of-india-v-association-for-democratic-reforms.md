## Why the case remains central

The judgment constitutionalized the idea that a voter cannot make a meaningful choice without basic information about candidates. Later affidavit rules, disclosure formats, and Election Commission portals all sit in its shadow.

## Long-term impact

This remains the opening case for candidate transparency, the right to know in elections, and every later argument that disclosure is part of constitutional democracy rather than campaign policy.
