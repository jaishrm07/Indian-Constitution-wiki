## Why the case remains central

The judgment is the bridge between voter-information cases and disqualification doctrine. It accepts that criminalisation of politics is a constitutional problem, but it leaves the architecture of disqualification to the legislature unless the Constitution itself says otherwise.

## Long-term impact

The case still anchors later debates about candidate affidavits, criminal antecedents, and the limits of judicially crafted eligibility rules in a representative democracy.
