## Why the reference remains central

The opinion remains the classic statement that privilege is real, but not absolute. Courts may not interfere with mere irregularity inside the House, yet they can examine illegality and constitutional breach.

## Long-term impact

Later privilege cases repeatedly return to this reference when drawing the line between internal legislative autonomy and judicial review, including modern disputes over discipline, contempt, and constitutional limits.
