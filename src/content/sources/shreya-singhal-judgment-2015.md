accessNotes: Two-judge bench judgment (J. Chelameswar and R.F. Nariman JJ.) delivered on 24 March 2015. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation AIR 2015 SC 1523 or (2015) 5 SCC 1. The Court struck down Section 66A of the IT Act in its entirety as unconstitutional for vagueness and overbreadth, found Section 69A and the Blocking Rules constitutionally valid subject to procedural safeguards, and read down Section 79 to apply only to cases where a court order or government notice has been issued. The judgment draws extensively on the three-category framework (discussion, advocacy, incitement) from Romesh Thappar v. State of Madras (1950).
description: Official Supreme Court judgment striking down Section 66A of the Information Technology Act 2000 as an unconstitutional restriction on online speech, establishing that the constitutional guarantee of free expression under Article 19(1)(a) applies equally to the internet, and articulating the distinction between mere advocacy and punishable incitement.
