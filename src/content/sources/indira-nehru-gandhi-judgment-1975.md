accessNotes: >-
  Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 7 November 1975. Retrieve
  via eSCR portal using the citation AIR 1975 SC 2299 or [1975] Supp SCC 1.
  The Court struck down Article 329A inserted by the Constitution
  (Thirty-Ninth Amendment) Act 1975, which had purported to retrospectively
  validate the election of the Prime Minister and to place such election disputes
  beyond the jurisdiction of any court. This was the first concrete application
  of the basic structure doctrine from Kesavananda Bharati (1973) to actually
  invalidate a constitutional amendment. The majority held that free and fair
  elections, the rule of law, and democracy are basic features of the
  Constitution that cannot be abrogated even through the amendment procedure
  in Article 368. The judgment is also significant for striking down Clause 4
  of the 39th Amendment, which sought to immunise the Prime Minister's election
  from judicial review.
description: >-
  Official Supreme Court judgment in which a five-judge bench struck down
  Article 329A (Clause 4 of the Thirty-Ninth Amendment) as violating the basic
  structure of the Constitution, being the first instance of the basic structure
  doctrine actually invalidating a constitutional amendment and affirming that
  free and fair elections are a fundamental constitutional value beyond
  Parliament's power to abrogate. The ruling established the principle that
  retrospective legislation designed to nullify a specific pending court
  judgment offends the separation of powers and basic structure.
