accessNotes: |
  Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 19 October 2006. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (2006) 8 SCC 212 or AIR 2007 SC 71. The Court upheld the constitutional validity of Article 16(4A) (inserted by the 77th Amendment), which permits the state to make provision for reservation in matters of promotion for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes. However, the bench imposed three conditions: the state must collect and place before the court quantifiable data demonstrating (i) the backwardness of the class, (ii) the inadequacy of its representation in public employment, and (iii) that overall administrative efficiency is maintained as required by Article 335. The Court did not apply the "creamy layer" exclusion to SCs and STs but left that question open; the roster point system for consequential seniority was upheld subject to the stated conditions.
