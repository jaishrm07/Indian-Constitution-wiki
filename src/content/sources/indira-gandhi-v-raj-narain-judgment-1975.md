accessNotes: Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 7 November 1975. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation AIR 1975 SC 2299 or [1975] Supp SCC 1. The Court struck down Article 329A inserted by the Constitution (Thirty-Ninth Amendment) Act 1975, which had purported to validate the election of the Prime Minister retrospectively and to place such disputes beyond the jurisdiction of courts. This case marked the first concrete application of the basic structure doctrine (articulated in Kesavananda Bharati in 1973) to actually invalidate a constitutional amendment. The majority held that free and fair elections and the rule of law are basic features of the Constitution that cannot be abrogated by Parliament even through the amendment procedure in Article 368.
description: Official Supreme Court judgment in which a five-judge bench struck down Article 329A (inserted by the Thirty-Ninth Amendment) as violating the basic structure of the Constitution, marking the first instance of the basic structure doctrine being used to invalidate a constitutional amendment and affirming free and fair elections as a fundamental constitutional value.
