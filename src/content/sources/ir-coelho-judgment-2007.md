accessNotes: Nine-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 11 January 2007. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (2007) 2 SCC 1 or AIR 2007 SC 861. The Court unanimously held that laws inserted into the Ninth Schedule on or after 24 April 1973 (the date of the Kesavananda Bharati judgment) are open to challenge on the ground that they damage or destroy the basic structure of the Constitution. Article 31B does not confer absolute immunity on such post-Kesavananda insertions. The test is whether the amendment that inserted the law into the Ninth Schedule abrogates or abridges a fundamental right that is itself part of the basic structure; if it does, the insertion is unconstitutional. Laws inserted before 24 April 1973 remain fully protected by Article 31B.
description: Official Supreme Court judgment in which a nine-judge bench unanimously ruled that laws placed in the Ninth Schedule after the Kesavananda Bharati judgment (24 April 1973) do not enjoy absolute immunity under Article 31B and are subject to challenge on basic structure grounds, while pre-1973 Ninth Schedule insertions remain constitutionally protected.
