accessNotes: Ten-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 19 February 1970. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation AIR 1970 SC 564 or [1970] 3 SCR 530. By a 10:1 majority the Court struck down the Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Ordinance 1969, which had nationalised fourteen major commercial banks. The critical doctrinal contribution of the case is its rejection of the compartmentalised approach to fundamental rights laid down in A.K. Gopalan v. State of Madras (1950). The Court adopted an integrated rights review, holding that the real effect or direct operation of state action on the fundamental rights of citizens must be examined, even if the law formally targets only one article. This "real effect test" was a precursor to the even broader rights synthesis achieved in Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India (1978).
description: Official Supreme Court judgment in which a ten-judge bench struck down the Banking Companies (Acquisition) Ordinance 1969 by a 10:1 majority, overturning the compartmentalised reading of fundamental rights from Gopalan and establishing the real effect test that requires courts to examine the true impact of state action across all applicable fundamental rights provisions.
