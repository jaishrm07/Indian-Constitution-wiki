accessNotes: Nine-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 6 October 1993. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation AIR 1994 SC 268 or (1993) 4 SCC 441. The majority opinion overruled the First Judges Case (S.P. Gupta, 1981) and held that the opinion of the Chief Justice of India, arrived at after consulting the two senior-most puisne judges of the Supreme Court, must ordinarily be binding on the executive in matters of appointment and transfer of judges.
description: Official Supreme Court judgment establishing the collegium system for judicial appointments and transfers, holding that the Chief Justice of India's primacy over the executive is constitutionally mandated and that the CJI must consult the two senior-most judges of the Supreme Court before tendering an opinion to the government.
