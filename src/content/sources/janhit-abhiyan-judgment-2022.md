accessNotes: >-
  Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 7 November 2022 by a 3:2
  majority. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (2022) 15 SCC 1. The
  majority (Maheshwari, Trivedi and Bela Trivedi JJ.) upheld the Constitution
  (One Hundred and Third Amendment) Act 2019, which inserted Articles 15(6) and
  16(6) to permit up to 10 per cent reservation for Economically Weaker Sections
  (EWS) outside the SC/ST/OBC creamy-layer framework and above the existing
  50 per cent ceiling established in Indra Sawhney (1992). The dissent (Ravindra
  Bhat and Lalit JJ.) held that the amendment violated the basic structure by
  excluding SCs, STs and OBCs from EWS benefits. The case is central to any
  analysis of whether the 50 per cent ceiling is part of basic structure and
  whether economic criteria alone can justify reservation.
description: >-
  Official Supreme Court judgment in which a five-judge bench upheld by a 3:2
  majority the 103rd Constitutional Amendment permitting 10 per cent EWS
  reservation, holding that economic criteria as a standalone classification for
  affirmative action does not violate the basic structure and that the 50 per
  cent ceiling from Indra Sawhney is not an inviolable basic structure rule. The
  two dissenting judges found that excluding SC/ST/OBC communities from EWS
  benefits constituted unconstitutional discrimination and violated basic
  structure.
