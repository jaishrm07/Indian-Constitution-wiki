accessNotes: Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 28 September 2018 (majority 4:1). Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (2019) 11 SCC 1. The majority (Dipak Misra CJ, R.F. Nariman, A.M. Khanwilkar, and D.Y. Chandrachud JJ) held that the exclusion of women aged 10–50 from the Sabarimala temple violated Articles 14, 15, 17, and 25(1) of the Constitution and struck down Rule 3(b) of the Kerala Hindu Places of Public Worship (Authorisation of Entry) Rules, 1965. Justice Indu Malhotra dissented, holding that the courts ought not to interfere in matters of religious faith and practice where a significant section of devotees holds a sincere belief. Following review petitions and conflicting outcomes in subsequent women's entry matters, a nine-judge bench reference on the scope and ambit of the essential religious practices doctrine (along with related references from Bohra and mosque-entry cases) remains pending as of 2024.
description: Supreme Court judgment by a five-judge Constitution Bench (4:1) permitting women of all ages to enter the Sabarimala temple, holding that the practice of excluding women aged 10–50 was unconstitutional. Justice Indu Malhotra's notable dissent argued that the court should not adjudicate matters of religious faith absent a substantial element of social reform. The decision triggered a broader nine-judge reference on essential religious practices that is still pending.
