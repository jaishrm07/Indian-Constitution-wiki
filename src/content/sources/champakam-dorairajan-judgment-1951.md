accessNotes: >-
  Supreme Court judgment delivered on 9 April 1951. Retrieve via eSCR portal
  using the citation AIR 1951 SC 226 or [1951] SCR 525. The Court struck down
  the Communal Government Order issued by the Madras government that reserved
  seats in state medical and engineering colleges on the basis of religion, race
  and caste, holding it void as conflicting with Article 29(2) which prohibits
  denial of admission to state-aided educational institutions on grounds of
  religion, race, caste, language or any of them. The judgment directly
  triggered the Constitution (First Amendment) Act 1951, which inserted
  Article 15(4) empowering the State to make special provisions for the
  advancement of socially and educationally backward classes or for SCs and
  STs, thereby providing the constitutional basis for caste-based reservations
  in education.
description: >-
  Official Supreme Court judgment striking down a communal reservation order for
  medical and engineering college seats as violating Article 29(2), establishing
  that reservation purely on caste or religious grounds in education was
  constitutionally impermissible under the original Constitution. The ruling
  directly prompted Parliament to enact the First Constitutional Amendment
  inserting Article 15(4), which became the foundational provision for
  reservation in educational institutions.
