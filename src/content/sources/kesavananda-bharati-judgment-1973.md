The judgment runs to over 700 pages across multiple opinions; the operative summary signed by nine judges articulates the core holding. Key portions include the majority's identification of basic structure elements (supremacy of the Constitution, republican and democratic form of government, secular character, separation of powers, federalism, unity and integrity of India) and the overruling of Golak Nath v. State of Punjab (1967).
description: Official Supreme Court judgment holding that although Parliament may amend any provision of the Constitution under Article 368, it cannot alter or destroy the basic structure or essential features of the Constitution, establishing a permanent judicial check on unlimited constituent power.
