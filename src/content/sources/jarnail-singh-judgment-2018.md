accessNotes: Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 26 September 2018. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (2018) 10 SCC 396 or AIR 2018 SC 4729. The bench partially reversed M. Nagaraj v. Union of India (2006) on the backwardness condition, holding that the state is not required to collect fresh quantifiable data to demonstrate the backwardness of SCs/STs before providing reservation in promotions — the inclusion of a community in the Presidential Lists under Articles 341 and 342 is itself sufficient proof of backwardness. The remaining two Nagaraj conditions — inadequacy of representation (which must still be demonstrated with quantifiable data for the specific cadre) and preservation of administrative efficiency under Article 335 — were retained. The 50% ceiling continues to apply. The bench declined to refer Nagaraj to a seven-judge bench for reconsideration and also noted, without deciding, the question of creamy layer exclusion from SC/ST promotion reservations.
description: Official Supreme Court judgment in which a five-judge Constitution Bench partially reversed M. Nagaraj (2006) by holding that states need not produce fresh data on backwardness for SCs/STs before granting reservation in promotions, as constitutional schedule listing is itself evidence of backwardness, while retaining the requirements of demonstrating inadequacy of representation with quantifiable data and maintaining administrative efficiency.
