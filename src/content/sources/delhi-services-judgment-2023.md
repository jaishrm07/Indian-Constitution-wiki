accessNotes: Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 11 May 2023 (unanimous). Retrieve via eSCR portal. The bench (D.Y. Chandrachud CJ, M.R. Shah, Krishna Murari, Hima Kohli, and P.S. Narasimha JJ) held that "services" — specifically transfers and postings of Group A officers serving under the Delhi government — fall within Entry 41 of List II (State List) read with Article 239AA, and that the elected Council of Ministers of Delhi has control over such officers, subject to the exceptions for police, public order, and land. The court reasoned that without accountability of the civil services to the elected executive, representative democracy becomes illusory. Parliament responded by enacting the Government of National Capital Territory of Delhi (Amendment) Act 2023, which was subsequently given constitutional status through the Constitution (One Hundred and Sixth Amendment) Act 2023, effectively restoring the Union's control over services in Delhi and overriding the Supreme Court's ruling.
description: Unanimous five-judge Constitution Bench judgment holding that the elected government of Delhi has legislative and executive authority over civil services (Group A officers) including transfers and postings, ensuring bureaucratic accountability to the elected executive. Parliament's response through the 106th Constitutional Amendment, passed within months of the ruling, transferred the services jurisdiction back to the Union, making this judgment an important episode in the ongoing contest over federal balance in Delhi's governance.
