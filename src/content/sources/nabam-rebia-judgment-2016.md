accessNotes: Five-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 13 July 2016. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (2016) 8 SCC 1 or AIR 2016 SC 3356. The case arose from the constitutional crisis in Arunachal Pradesh in 2015–16, where the Governor advanced the Assembly session and convened it on his own initiative while disqualification proceedings against rebel legislators were pending before the Speaker. The Court held unanimously that the Governor does not have an independent power to convene or advance a session of the Legislative Assembly contrary to the aid and advice of the Council of Ministers, and that such action to pre-empt anti-defection proceedings was constitutionally impermissible. The judgment clarifies the interplay between the Tenth Schedule's anti-defection regime, the Speaker's adjudicatory role, and the Governor's Article 174 powers.
description: Official Supreme Court judgment in which a five-judge bench unanimously held that the Governor of Arunachal Pradesh exceeded his constitutional authority by summoning the Assembly session contrary to the advice of the Council of Ministers and in a manner designed to defeat pending anti-defection proceedings against rebel legislators, reinforcing the limits of gubernatorial discretion and the primacy of the aid and advice convention.
