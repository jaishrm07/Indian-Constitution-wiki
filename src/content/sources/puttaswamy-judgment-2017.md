accessNotes: Nine-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 24 August 2017 (unanimous on the core right; six separate concurring opinions). Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (2017) 10 SCC 1. The nine-judge bench unanimously held that privacy is a fundamental right intrinsic to life and liberty under Article 21 and also arises from Articles 14 and 19. The judgment explicitly overruled the eight-judge bench in M.P. Sharma v. Satish Chandra (1954) and the two-judge bench in Kharak Singh v. State of U.P. (1962) to the extent they held privacy is not a fundamental right. Justice D.Y. Chandrachud's lead opinion sets out the three-pronged proportionality framework for evaluating permissible State interference with privacy.
description: Official Supreme Court judgment unanimously recognising privacy as a fundamental right under the Constitution, overruling earlier precedents that denied this right, and establishing a proportionality-based framework — requiring a legitimate aim, a rational nexus, and the least restrictive means — for evaluating State intrusions on the right to privacy.
