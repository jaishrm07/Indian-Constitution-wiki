accessNotes: Eleven-judge Constitution Bench judgment delivered on 31 October 2002. Retrieve via eSCR portal using the citation (2002) 8 SCC 481. The bench was constituted to resolve conflicting precedents from St. Stephen's College v. University of Delhi and Unni Krishnan, J.P. v. State of Andhra Pradesh. Multiple separate opinions were delivered; the key holdings concern the distinction between permissible regulation and impermissible state takeover of management, the unit for determining minority status (held to be the state, not the country), and the respective autonomy of minority and non-minority private unaided institutions. The judgment significantly restructured the regulatory framework for private higher education in India.
description: Official Supreme Court eleven-judge Constitution Bench judgment holding that minority institutions enjoy a constitutionally protected right under Article 30 to establish and administer educational institutions with meaningful autonomy over admissions and management, that private unaided non-minority institutions possess comparable autonomy under Article 19(1)(g), and that minority status must be assessed at the state level; the judgment drew a line between permissible educational regulation and unconstitutional interference with institutional autonomy.
