## Why this topic matters

The Constitution addresses pluralism through specific guarantees, especially in language, culture, and education. Minority rights are therefore not peripheral but part of constitutional structure.

## How to read the topic

Readers should connect Articles 28, 29, and 30 with equality, religious freedom, and disputes over the permissible scope of state regulation.

## Key cases

- [St. Stephen's College v. University of Delhi](../cases/st-stephens-college) is the classic minority-college autonomy case.
- [P.A. Inamdar v. State of Maharashtra](../cases/pa-inamdar) explains the limits of compelled state quota policy in unaided professional institutions.
- [Bijoe Emmanuel v. State of Kerala](../cases/bijoe-emmanuel) shows how conscience and minority freedom can protect respectful nonconformity.
