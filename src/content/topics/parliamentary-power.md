## Why this topic matters

Parliament is central to constitutional change, but its authority is not self-defining. Questions about amendment power, procedure, and structural limits shape some of the most important constitutional conflicts in Indian public law.

## How to read the topic

Start with the constitutional position of Parliament and then move to Article 368. The most important reading path is through amendment-era cases and the debate over whether constituent power can be broad without being unlimited.

## Key cases

- [P.V. Narasimha Rao v. State (CBI/SPE)](../cases/pv-narasimha-rao) is the core parliamentary-privilege case on speech, vote, and immunity.
- [Raja Ram Pal v. Hon'ble Speaker, Lok Sabha](../cases/raja-ram-pal) shows that legislative privilege does not block all judicial review.
- [Kihoto Hollohan v. Zachillhu](../cases/kihoto-hollohan) remains relevant where parliamentary discipline overlaps with constitutional control.

## Current significance

The current significance of this topic is the continuing tension between Parliament's amending power, its privilege claims, and the Court's insistence that constitutional structure still sets outer limits.
