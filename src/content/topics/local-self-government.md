## Why this topic matters

The Constitution does not treat democratic representation as exclusively national or state-based. Local self-government expands the constitutional imagination of participation and administration.

## How to read the topic

Readers should move between the Seventy-Third and Seventy-Fourth Amendments, the Eleventh and Twelfth Schedules, and the broader idea of devolved constitutional governance.

## Key cases

- [Kishansing Tomar v. Municipal Corporation of the City of Ahmedabad](../cases/kishansing-tomar) is the leading case on timely municipal elections.
- [K. Krishna Murthy v. Union of India](../cases/k-krishna-murthy) remains central to local-body reservations and representative design.

## Current significance

This topic is now closely tied to election timing, reservation design, and administrative capacity in local institutions. The live discussions on [local body election delays and State Election Commission independence](../current-affairs/local-body-election-delays-and-sec-independence), [delimitation and representation](../current-affairs/delimitation-and-representation), and [women reservation and delimitation](../current-affairs/women-reservation-and-delimitation) show why local democracy remains constitutionally active.
