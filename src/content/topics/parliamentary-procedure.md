## Why this topic matters

Many constitutional crises are procedural before they are ideological. Anti-defection disputes, recognition of party splits, the role of the Speaker, and the timing of floor tests can decide whether representative government remains stable or becomes manipulable.

## How to read the topic

Read this topic through the Tenth Schedule, the powers of presiding officers, and the cases that decide when courts will intervene in legislative process. It often overlaps with federalism because state-level instability becomes a constitutional problem quickly.
