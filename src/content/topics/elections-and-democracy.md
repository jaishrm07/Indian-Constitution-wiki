## Why this topic matters

Some constitutional election questions are about institutional administration, but others are about democracy more directly: who votes, who gets represented, how constituencies are drawn, and when representative change actually takes effect.

## How to read the topic

Read Articles 324 to 329 together with Article 326 and the amendment history around franchise, delimitation, and representation. Current-affairs pages on delimitation and legislative reservation belong here because they change the democratic map, not just electoral procedure.

## Key cases

- [People's Union for Civil Liberties v. Union of India (Candidate Disclosure)](../cases/pucl-candidate-disclosure) shows why voter choice depends on basic candidate disclosure.
- [Lily Thomas v. Union of India](../cases/lily-thomas) explains why criminal conviction cannot be hidden behind delayed disqualification.
- [Kishansing Tomar v. Municipal Corporation of the City of Ahmedabad](../cases/kishansing-tomar) makes timely local elections a constitutional obligation.
- [Anoop Baranwal v. Union of India](../cases/anoop-baranwal) sits at the center of the Election Commission independence debate.
