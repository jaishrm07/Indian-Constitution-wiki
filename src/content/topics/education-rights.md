## Why this topic matters

Education rights show how the Constitution can move from aspiration to enforceable obligation. The shift from directive principle language to Article 21A changed the constitutional vocabulary of schooling, state duty, and children's rights.

## How to read the topic

Start with Article 21A, then read it alongside Article 45 and the broader equality and dignity logic that shapes access to education. Amendment history matters because the rights framework was not fully present from the beginning.

## Key cases

- [Unni Krishnan, J.P. v. State of Andhra Pradesh](../cases/unni-krishnan) is the bridge between education as welfare and education as a constitutional right.
- [St. Stephen's College v. University of Delhi](../cases/st-stephens-college) shows how minority educational autonomy interacts with admission policy.
- [T.M.A. Pai Foundation v. State of Karnataka](../cases/tma-pai-foundation) remains foundational for private and minority education.
- [P.A. Inamdar v. State of Maharashtra](../cases/pa-inamdar) sets the modern boundary on compelled reservations in private unaided institutions.

## Current significance

Education rights now sit at the intersection of access, merit, autonomy, and regulation. The topic remains live whenever admissions policy, minority character, or state regulation is contested in court or in public debate.
