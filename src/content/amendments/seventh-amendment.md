## Why it matters

The Seventh Amendment is the constitutional hinge between the early republic and the modern territorial state. It matters because the Constitution's federal logic only becomes practical once the state map, legislative representation, and territorial categories are aligned.

## Reading note

Read this amendment with Articles 1 to 4 and the representation Articles together. It is not just a technical update; it is the moment the Constitution's territorial design became usable at national scale.
