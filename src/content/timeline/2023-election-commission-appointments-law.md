## What happened

Parliament passed the Chief Election Commissioner and Other Election Commissioners (Appointment, Conditions of Service and Term of Office) Act, 2023 in December 2023, exercising the legislative power under Article 324(2) which had lain dormant since the Constitution came into force. The Act established a Selection Committee comprising the Prime Minister as Chairperson, a Cabinet Minister nominated by the Prime Minister, and the Leader of the Opposition in the Lok Sabha. This was a direct legislative response to the Supreme Court's direction in Anoop Baranwal, which had included the Chief Justice of India in the interim selection panel. The Act received Presidential assent on 28 December 2023.

## Why it mattered constitutionally

The legislation confirmed that Parliament's power under Article 324(2) is plenary once exercised, and that the Court's interim arrangements in Anoop Baranwal were exactly that — interim. By excluding the Chief Justice of India from the selection committee, Parliament signalled that it did not accept the judiciary's assessment of what institutional independence under Article 324 requires. The Act reignited constitutional debate about whether a selection committee dominated by the ruling executive adequately protects the Election Commission's independence, and whether that design is itself vulnerable to basic-structure challenge.

## Its legacy

The 2023 Act became the focus of fresh petitions questioning whether a statutory design that gives the executive an effective majority on the selection committee satisfies the constitutional independence requirement that Anoop Baranwal identified. The episode as a whole — from the Supreme Court's direction to Parliament's response — became the defining example of the post-NJAC dialogue between the Court and Parliament on the design of independent constitutional institutions.
