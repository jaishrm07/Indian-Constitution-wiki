This is one of the most significant representation reforms in the modern Constitution. It is also a reminder that some amendments change the text immediately but only become operational after later constitutional conditions are met.

## What happened

The One Hundred and Sixth Amendment, passed during a special session of Parliament in September 2023, inserted Articles 330A, 332A, and 334A into the Constitution. These articles reserve one-third of all seats in the Lok Sabha, state legislative assemblies, and the National Capital Territory of Delhi's Legislative Assembly for women, with a portion of the reserved seats earmarked for women from Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes. However, Article 334A conditions the commencement of these reservations on the completion of a delimitation exercise following the first census conducted after the amendment's enactment, meaning the reservation cannot take effect until a census and subsequent delimitation are carried out.

## Why it mattered constitutionally

The amendment fulfilled a long-standing political and constitutional commitment that had been introduced and lapsed multiple times in Parliament since the 1990s, making it one of the most protracted pieces of constitutional reform in Indian history. It established a new model of representation-based affirmative action within elected bodies, distinct from the existing reservation frameworks for Scheduled Castes and Tribes and for OBCs in local bodies. The delimitation-linked commencement condition made the effective date of the amendment hostage to future political decisions about when a census would be conducted and delimitation carried out, introducing a structural delay into the constitutional text itself.

## Its legacy

The amendment's deferred operation has kept the question of women's representation constitutionally present but practically suspended, with the timing of the required census and delimitation becoming both a governance and a political issue. It represents the most significant expansion of the constitutional framework for political representation since the reservation of SC and ST seats at independence, and its eventual operationalisation will reshape electoral arithmetic in every state legislature and the Lok Sabha.
