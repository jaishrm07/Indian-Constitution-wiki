The ruling is now the central constitutional reference point for future disputes over Governors, assent, and state legislative autonomy.

## What happened

The State of Tamil Nadu approached the Supreme Court after the Governor had withheld assent to a substantial number of bills passed by the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly, in some cases for periods of years, without returning them for reconsideration or reserving them for the President's consideration. A bench of the Supreme Court, in a ruling delivered in April 2025, held that the Governor's options under Article 200 are constituted and limited by the text of the Constitution itself: the Governor must assent, withhold assent and return for reconsideration, or reserve for the President's consideration — and cannot simply hold bills in an indefinite limbo. The Court applied Article 142 to deem the withheld bills as having received assent, providing immediate relief to Tamil Nadu.

## Why it mattered constitutionally

The ruling clarified that Articles 200 and 201 impose constitutional duties on the Governor, not merely options, and that the exercise of those duties is subject to constitutional timelines rooted in the requirement of responsible government. It engaged the larger structural question of how a constitutional head, who acts on ministerial advice, can legitimately use an assent power to obstruct the functioning of a state legislature elected under the same constitution. The judgment reinforced the principle from Bommai and the Delhi governance cases that federal balance requires that state legislative authority be respected and not be systematically undermined through the exercise of constitutional offices.

## Its legacy

The Tamil Nadu Governor ruling immediately became the leading case on gubernatorial assent powers, and its reasoning was cited in similar disputes involving Governors and Lieutenant Governors in other states. It placed a constitutional floor under the conduct of Governors in relation to state legislation, converting what had previously been a political and constitutional convention into an enforceable legal duty, and strengthened the hand of states facing deliberate executive obstruction through the assent mechanism.
