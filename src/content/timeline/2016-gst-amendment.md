The amendment is a major example of economic reform achieved through constitutional redesign rather than ordinary legislation alone.

## What happened

The One Hundred and First Amendment, passed by Parliament and ratified by the required number of state legislatures in 2016, replaced the fragmented indirect tax structure with a unified Goods and Services Tax framework. It inserted Articles 246A, 269A, and 279A into the Constitution, created a GST Council to govern rate-setting jointly, and stripped both Parliament and state legislatures of their pre-existing exclusive competence over most indirect taxes. The amendment was the product of over a decade of inter-governmental negotiation and required states to cede significant fiscal power to a jointly governed regime. It came into operational effect on 1 July 2017.

## Why it mattered constitutionally

Before the amendment, the Constitution sharply divided taxing powers between the Union List and the State List, making a single national indirect tax structurally impossible without constitutional change. Article 246A created a concurrent taxing power over GST, a category that had no predecessor in the original constitutional design. The GST Council — a body where the Union holds a fixed vote share and states collectively hold a majority — introduced a new form of cooperative federalism that operates outside the classical division of powers in Part XI. The amendment also raised questions about whether a constitutional body with binding rate-setting powers could itself be subject to judicial review.

## Its legacy

The GST framework became the reference point for subsequent debates about fiscal federalism, showing that constitutional amendment could be used to create intergovernmental coordination bodies rather than merely to redistribute powers. Disputes between states and the Union over compensation, cess allocation, and council voting have reinforced the view that the GST amendment shifted the constitutional terrain of Centre-state fiscal relations in ways that ordinary legislative design could not have achieved.
