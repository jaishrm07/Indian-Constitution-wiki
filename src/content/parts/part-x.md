## What this Part contains

Part X consists of Articles 244 and 244A and governs administration of the Scheduled Areas and Tribal Areas listed in the Fifth and Sixth Schedules to the Constitution. Article 244 applies the Fifth Schedule to Scheduled Areas in states other than Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, and Mizoram, and applies the Sixth Schedule to tribal areas in those four north-eastern states. The Fifth Schedule establishes Tribes Advisory Councils in states with Scheduled Areas and vests the Governor with special powers to direct that Central and State laws shall not apply to a Scheduled Area or shall apply subject to modifications, while the Sixth Schedule creates Autonomous District Councils with legislative, executive, and judicial powers in the north-eastern tribal areas. Article 244A, inserted in 1969, enables Parliament to form an autonomous state comprising tribal areas in Assam with its own legislature and council of ministers within the state, a provision that has been invoked in the ongoing political processes around Bodoland.

## Key constitutional provisions

- **Article 244** — Applies the Fifth Schedule to Scheduled Areas in states outside the four north-eastern states and the Sixth Schedule to tribal areas in Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, and Mizoram, establishing two distinct constitutional regimes for tribal governance.
- **Article 244A** — Authorises Parliament to form by law an autonomous state within Assam comprising tribal areas, which may have its own legislature and council of ministers, with provisions for legislative competence and financial arrangements.

## Key cases

**Samatha v. State of Andhra Pradesh** — The Supreme Court held that alienation of tribal land in Scheduled Areas to non-tribals and to non-tribal corporations for industrial purposes was unconstitutional as it violated the Fifth Schedule’s protective framework and the Governor’s obligation to ensure the well-being of tribal communities, a landmark ruling for forest and mineral rights in tribal areas.

**State of Andhra Pradesh v. Lavu Narendranath** — The Court examined the scope of the Governor’s special powers under the Fifth Schedule to adapt laws for Scheduled Areas and affirmed that the Governor’s authority in this domain is independent and not subject to ordinary ministerial advice in the same way as other gubernatorial functions.

**Chebrolu Leela Prasad Rao v. State of Andhra Pradesh** — The Court invalidated a government order restricting all teacher posts in Scheduled Area schools to Scheduled Tribe candidates, holding it violated Articles 14 and 16, but also engaged extensively with what the Fifth Schedule permits states to do to protect tribal interests in public employment.

## Current significance

Land rights, forest rights, and mineral extraction in Fifth Schedule areas remain among the most contested constitutional questions, particularly as Scheduled Area states grant mining leases and industrial licences that displace tribal communities. The Forest Rights Act 2006 and the PESA Act 1996 operate alongside the Fifth Schedule and generate frequent litigation about the priority of rights in Scheduled Areas. Implementation of the Sixth Schedule in north-eastern states and the demands of various tribal groups for greater autonomous powers under Article 244A continue to be politically and constitutionally live issues in Assam and Meghalaya.
