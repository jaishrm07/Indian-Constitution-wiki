## What this Part contains

Part XIV spans Articles 308 to 323 and governs civil services under the Union and the States, as well as the Public Service Commissions that regulate recruitment and conditions of service. Article 309 empowers Parliament and state legislatures to regulate the recruitment and conditions of service of persons serving the Union or a state, while Article 310 establishes the doctrine of pleasure under which civil servants hold office at the pleasure of the President or the Governor. Article 311, however, qualifies the pleasure doctrine significantly by requiring that a civil servant shall not be dismissed or removed by an authority subordinate to the appointing authority, and shall be given a reasonable opportunity of hearing before any penalty of dismissal, removal, or reduction in rank is imposed. The constitutional guarantee of reasoned disciplinary procedure in Article 311 has generated an enormous volume of service jurisprudence.

## Key constitutional provisions

- **Article 309** — Empowers Parliament and state legislatures to regulate the recruitment and conditions of service of persons appointed to public services and posts, providing the constitutional basis for the Central and State Civil Services Rules.
- **Article 310** — States that persons serving the Union or a state hold office during the pleasure of the President or the Governor, subject to any express provision of the Constitution, establishing the contractual and constitutional basis for government service tenure.
- **Article 311** — Qualifies the pleasure doctrine by requiring that no civil servant be dismissed or removed by an authority subordinate to the appointing authority, and that no dismissal, removal, or reduction in rank be imposed without giving the civil servant a reasonable opportunity to be heard.
- **Article 315** — Requires the establishment of Public Service Commissions for the Union and for each state, constitutionally entrenching independent recruitment bodies as a safeguard against political patronage in public employment.
- **Article 320** — Sets out the functions of Public Service Commissions including conducting examinations, advising on recruitment, and being consulted on disciplinary matters, making their role a constitutional requirement rather than merely statutory.

## Key cases

**Parshotam Lal Dhingra v. Union of India** — The Supreme Court laid down the foundational principle that the constitutional protection under Article 311 attaches only when there is a forfeiture of substantive rank or office, and that reversion to a lower post as a disciplinary measure triggers the procedural safeguards, a ruling that shaped the entire field of service law for decades.

**Union of India v. Tulsiram Patel** — A five-judge bench reinterpreted the exceptions to Article 311(2), particularly the exception where it is not reasonably practicable to hold an enquiry, and affirmed that these exceptions must be construed narrowly and that their invocation is subject to judicial review.

**State of Orissa v. Dr. (Miss) Binapani Dei** — The Court held that the rules of natural justice and Article 311’s procedural protections apply even to provisional and temporary arrangements in service matters, expanding the protective scope of Part XIV beyond permanent appointments.

## Current significance

Contractualisation and lateral entry into senior civil service positions, along with the use of deputation and performance-linked service schemes, raises constitutional questions about whether such arrangements satisfy or circumvent the protection of Article 311. The constitutional obligation to consult the Union Public Service Commission under Article 320 in disciplinary proceedings is frequently litigated in service courts and High Courts. Reservation in promotions for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes in government services — governed by Article 16(4A) read with service rules under Part XIV — continues to be an active area of constitutional adjudication including the pending question of whether quantifiable data on inadequate representation must be freshly collected for each round of promotions.
