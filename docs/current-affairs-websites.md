# Current Affairs Websites

This project should not treat all “current affairs” websites as interchangeable. For constitutional current affairs, the right stack is layered:

1. official institutional source
2. structured legislative or judicial tracker
3. legal-news monitoring source

## Best sites to use

### Primary institutional sources

Use these first whenever they exist for the issue:

- India Code: https://www.indiacode.nic.in/
- Supreme Court of India: https://www.sci.gov.in/
- Judgments and Orders portal: https://judgments.ecourts.gov.in/
- eSCR: https://scr.sci.gov.in/
- Sansad: https://sansad.in/
- Parliament Digital Library: https://eparlib.sansad.in/
- Press Information Bureau archive: https://www.pib.gov.in/AllReleasem.aspx?MenuId=3&lang=1&reg=3
- Election Commission of India: https://eci.gov.in/
- eGazette: https://www.egazette.nic.in/

These are the sites that should anchor published current-affairs pages.

Why:

- Sansad exposes current bill status, dates of passage, assent dates, Gazette notification columns, and committee references on its bills pages.
- PIB has a dated archive filtered by ministry and day, which is useful for reconstructing official announcement chronology.
- The Supreme Court now also publishes official landmark judgment summaries in simple language, with links to the full judgment and, where available, hearing videos and transcripts.

## Best secondary websites

### 1. PRS Legislative Research

Website:

- https://prsindia.org/
- Bills tracker: https://prsindia.org/billtrack

Why use it:

- best structured legislative tracker outside official government websites
- useful for bill summaries, session alerts, and legislative context

How to use it:

- use for framing, chronology, and summaries
- confirm the operative legal text and status through Sansad, Gazette, or official law sources

### 2. Supreme Court Observer

Website:

- https://www.scobserver.in/

Why use it:

- especially strong for Constitution Bench coverage
- useful for case pages, hearing summaries, and pending constitutional questions

How to use it:

- use it to track major constitutional cases and understand where a matter is procedurally
- verify case documents and holdings against official court portals

### 3. Live Law

Website:

- https://www.livelaw.in/

Why use it:

- very fast for court hearing updates
- useful for spotting constitutional developments early

How to use it:

- use as an early-warning and monitoring source
- do not let it become the only source for consequential claims

### 4. Bar and Bench

Website:

- https://www.barandbench.com/

Why use it:

- useful for legal-news monitoring and judicial institution coverage
- can help surface constitutional litigation and institutional developments quickly

How to use it:

- use for monitoring and contextual coverage
- confirm critical facts against judgments, orders, or official institutional records

### 5. Vidhi Centre for Legal Policy

Website:

- https://vidhilegalpolicy.in/

Why use it:

- useful for deeper legal-policy analysis and reform context
- good when the issue intersects with institutional reform or structural public-law questions

How to use it:

- use for depth and background, not for the operative legal text

## Recommended current-affairs workflow

### For bills and structural reform

Use:

- Sansad
- PIB
- India Code / Legislative Department / Gazette
- PRS

### For constitutional litigation

Use:

- Supreme Court of India
- Judgments and Orders portal
- eSCR
- Supreme Court Observer
- Live Law or Bar and Bench for early monitoring

### For elections and institutional disputes

Use:

- ECI
- Sansad
- PIB
- Supreme Court sources if litigation begins

## What I would trust most

If we want one practical monitoring stack for the live layer, the best one is:

1. official sources listed above
2. PRS Legislative Research
3. Supreme Court Observer
4. Live Law
5. Bar and Bench

That gives us one official-first system plus three strong secondary trackers.

## Recommendation for this project

For the website itself:

- publish only when there is at least one official institutional source
- add PRS or Supreme Court Observer when they improve structure and readability
- use Live Law and Bar and Bench mainly for monitoring and discovery

That keeps the site fast on live developments without weakening the source standard.

For the operating cadence and review steps that turn those sources into published issue pages, use `docs/editorial-operations.md`.
